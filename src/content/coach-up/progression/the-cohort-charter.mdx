# The Cohort Charter

*A Coach Up cohort is three apprentices. That number is a design decision, and it comes with a small set of commitments the three make to each other. This is that charter.*

---

## Why three

Three is "small on purpose," and the purpose is real:

- **Three is what keeps the [weekly 1:1](../flows/weekly-1-1-flow) honest.** Mentorship at scale stops being mentorship. With three apprentices, Sam can actually watch closely enough to tell each one the true thing — which is [the entire product](../creed/what-coach-up-is-not).
- **Three is enough for a peer cohort, small enough to stay tight.** One apprentice has no peers. Ten has a class, not a cohort. Three is a group that can actually know each other's work.
- **It is real bandwidth, not manufactured scarcity.** The cap exists because honest mentorship has a real ceiling — not as a marketing trick.

## What each member owes the other two

The cohort isn't three people who happen to share a calendar. It's three people who agreed to a few things:

### Show up to the shared thread

Three apprentices, one channel. You owe it your presence — your wins, your problems, your questions. A cohort-mate's stuck Tuesday is a thing you can help with; your stuck Tuesday is a thing they can. A silent member quietly takes value from the cohort without putting any back.

### Be observable

In [Week 11](../curriculum/week-11) you observe a cohort-mate and one observes you. That only works if you let them see the real thing — the rough session, not the polished one. Being observable, including at your worst, is part of the deal.

### Trade honestly

Peer feedback only helps if it's real. You owe your cohort-mates the same honesty Sam owes you — specific, warm, true. "That looked great" when it didn't isn't kindness; it's withholding the thing they joined the cohort for.

### Protect the room

The cohort is a place to be coached in front of your peers — which only works if it's safe. What's shared in the cohort is the cohort's. You protect that, so the next person can be honest too.

## What the cohort is not

It's not a competition. Apprentices don't graduate *against* each other — [the Readiness Bar](the-readiness-bar) is an absolute standard, not a ranked one. All three can graduate; the cohort-mate who gets better fastest is a resource, not a threat. The cohort is the opposite of [the gatekept private group](../creed/what-coach-up-is-not) — it's a small room that's genuinely on your side.

> Three apprentices, by design — it's what keeps the mentorship honest. Show up to the thread, be observable, trade honest feedback, protect the room. The cohort is on your side; act like it.
