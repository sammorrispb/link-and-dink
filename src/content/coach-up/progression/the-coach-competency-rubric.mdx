# The Coach Competency Rubric

*[The Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) is the fast daily check — seven boxes, ten minutes. This rubric is the full picture: the six competencies that describe a whole coach, scored against at [graduation](../flows/graduation-flow) and revisited up [the ladder](the-coach-up-ladder).*

---

## Where the six come from

The rubric is built on the **International Sport Coaching Framework** from the International Council for Coaching Excellence (ICCE) — which defines the primary functions of coaching itself (see [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations)). Coach Up adapts those functions into six competencies, translated to a pickleball court and the L&D voice. Building the rubric on an established framework is deliberate: it means "ready" is anchored to what the coaching profession actually means by competence, not to one person's gut.

## The six competencies

For each, the apprentice self-scores honestly: *strong / solid / still thin*.

### 1. Set the vision and the focus

Can the coach design a session with one clear focus and frame the *why* so it lands? Do their sessions have direction, or do they drift? — *Built on: [Session Design Framework](../frameworks/session-design-framework), [The Frame](../vocabulary/term-the-frame).*

### 2. Shape the environment

Can the coach design constraints that make the skill the easy path — turn the dials, build the game that does the teaching? This is the central craft. — *Built on: [The Constraint Design Toolkit](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit), [The Coach as Environment Designer](../creed/the-coach-as-environment-designer).*

### 3. Build the relationships

Can the coach build trust with adult players — know them by name, coach the actual human in front of them, handle the hard conversations warmly and honestly? — *Built on: [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids), [Getting & Keeping Students](../systems/getting-and-keeping-students).*

### 4. Conduct the practice

Can the coach run a live session — hold both [Pillars](../frameworks/the-coach-up-pillars), diagnose with [the Hierarchy](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment), deliver feedback that lands? This is the rubric's biggest competency, the doing of it. — *Built on: [The Coach Up Pillars](../frameworks/the-coach-up-pillars), [Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment), [The Feedback Framework](../frameworks/the-feedback-framework).*

### 5. Read and adjust

Can the coach read the room in real time and turn a dial mid-session — adjust the design to what's actually happening, not freeze or push the plan through regardless? — *Built on: [Reading the Room](../frameworks/reading-the-room), [The Mixed-Level Playbook](../frameworks/the-mixed-level-playbook).*

### 6. Learn and reflect

Does the coach run honest debriefs, track running focuses, and act on the patterns — is the coach themselves still coachable? This competency is the engine of all the others. — *Built on: [The Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow), [The Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment), [The Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) belief #10.*

## How it's used

- **At graduation** — the apprentice self-scores all six honestly in [Week 12](../curriculum/week-12); it's a required input to the three-yes sign-off and pairs with [The Readiness Bar](the-readiness-bar).
- **Up the ladder** — the same six competencies describe a coach at every tier; what changes is the depth expected. A Lead Coach is strong across all six *and* can develop those competencies in others.
- **As a development map** — between graduations, "still thin" on a competency is just a pointer to which part of the handbook to go back to. The rubric tells you where to grow next.

## The honesty rule

This rubric is only worth anything if the self-score is honest. A flattering self-score isn't kindness — it robs you of the map. "Still thin" on a competency is not a failure; it's the most useful thing the rubric can tell you. Score it true.

> Six competencies — set the vision, shape the environment, build relationships, conduct practice, read and adjust, learn and reflect. Built on the ICCE framework, scored honestly. It's a map, not a verdict.
