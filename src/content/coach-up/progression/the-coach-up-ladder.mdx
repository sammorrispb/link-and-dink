# The Coach Up Ladder

*Coach Up is twelve weeks. Coaching is a career. The ladder is the path past graduation — so that growth has a shape instead of just stopping when the cohort ends.*

---

## What this is, and isn't

The ladder is a **path**, not a **timeline**. You move up a tier when you've earned it, not when the calendar says so. One coach reaches Lead Coach fast; another is still deepening at Coach for a long while — both are fine. The ladder exists so the growth has direction, not so it has a deadline.

What the ladder is *not*: a published comp grid. The tiers and what they unlock are transparent; the dollars are a one-on-one conversation, because every coach's mix of events, lessons, and clinics is different.

## The ladder at a glance

```
APPRENTICE      The 12-week cohort. Learning the craft under
                close mentorship. Reps from week two.
   │
   ▼  graduation — the three-yes sign-off
COACH           Runs their own events, lessons, and clinics.
                Full rev-share access. Mentorship continues, lighter.
   │
   ▼  a real book + the craft deepened
LEAD COACH      Mentors apprentices. Co-runs a cohort. May own
                a venue or program area.
   │
   ▼  proven as a developer of coaches
MASTER-TRACK    Senior. Runs cohorts, shapes the curriculum,
                stands in for Sam.
```

## Apprentice

The twelve-week cohort itself. You're learning the craft under the closest mentorship Coach Up offers — the [weekly 1:1](../flows/weekly-1-1-flow), the [debrief](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) after every session, the cohort of three. Real reps from week two; real, gentle stakes. **What unlocks the next tier:** graduation — the three-yes sign-off at [Week 12](../curriculum/week-12), measured against [The Readiness Bar](the-readiness-bar).

## Coach

You've graduated. You run your own events, lessons, and clinics — your [rate card](../vocabulary/term-rate-card) is yours, the [rev-share](../vocabulary/term-rev-share) structure applies, and the scaffolding of the apprenticeship is behind you. The mentorship relationship continues, but lighter — periodic check-ins, not a weekly 1:1. **What you're building here:** a real, sustainable practice — a book of students, a community around your events, the craft deepening with every honestly debriefed rep. **What unlocks the next tier:** a genuine, sustained practice *and* the craft deepened to the point where you could teach it — not just do it.

## Lead Coach

You start developing *other* coaches. You mentor apprentices, co-run a cohort alongside Sam, run [the weekly 1:1](../flows/weekly-1-1-flow) and the [debriefs](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) for new apprentices, and may own a venue or program area end to end. **What you're learning here:** the teacher's seat — running a debrief is a different skill than receiving one; asking the right question beats giving the answer. **What unlocks the next tier:** proven, repeated success as a developer of coaches — apprentices who graduated well because of your mentorship.

## Master-Track

Senior. You can run cohorts, you shape the handbook and the curriculum itself, and you can stand in for Sam — on fit conversations, on graduation decisions, on the brand. **What you're learning here:** the whole system, not just the craft — how the apprenticeship works as a thing, how Coach Up grows, the strategic picture. This is the ceiling of the *coach* ladder; beyond it is a different conversation entirely.

## What stays the same at every tier

The ladder changes *what you own*. It doesn't change *how you coach*. At every tier:

- The [Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) still drives the decisions.
- The two [Pillars](../frameworks/the-coach-up-pillars) are still the standard for every session.
- The [post-session self-assessment and debrief](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) still run, every session.
- You're still [the most coachable person on your court](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) — that one never graduates.

> Apprentice, Coach, Lead Coach, Master-Track. A path, not a timeline — you climb when you've earned it. What you own changes; how you coach doesn't.
