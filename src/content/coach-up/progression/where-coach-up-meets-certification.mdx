# Where Coach Up Meets Certification

*Coach Up is [not a certification](../creed/what-coach-up-is-not) — and it doesn't compete with one either. They do different jobs. This page is the full, plain position, so the relationship is never fuzzy.*

---

## What a certification is for

A certification — PPR (Professional Pickleball Registry), IPTPA (International Pickleball Teaching Professional Association) — is two real things:

1. **A body of content and a test** — the rules, the safety basics, a stock set of drills, a standardized assessment.
2. **A path to liability insurance** — and this is the part that genuinely matters. The moment you're running real, paid sessions, you need to be insured, and certification is the standard route there.

Both of those are useful. Neither of them is a coach.

## What a certification is not

A certification does not make you a coach. Knowing the content and being able to read a struggling adult across a court mid-rally are different skills — and the certification mostly tests the first one. The drills it teaches are stock; the pedagogy is generally light on the science of how people actually learn (see [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations) for what that science says, and where the cert bodies are thin on it).

That gap — between *knowing the content* and *having the craft* — is the entire reason Coach Up exists. The cert and the apprenticeship aren't rivals. They're the two halves: **get certified for the coverage; build the craft here.**

## Coach Up's position, plainly

- **Coach Up recommends you get certified** — once you're running real events, for the insurance. Not on day one; you'll get reps under Sam well before you ever need it.
- **Coach Up does not subsidize the cost.** You pay for your certification yourself. This is deliberate — it keeps the apprenticeship an apprenticeship, not a benefits package, and it keeps the decision yours.
- **Where Coach Up helps:** choosing the right pathway (PPR vs. IPTPA, which level, which format), study guidance and what to expect on the assessment, and connecting you with other certified coaches. The *decision and the support* are part of the apprenticeship; the *bill* is yours.
- **When the decision happens:** [Week 10](../curriculum/week-10) — the business-of-coaching week — is where you and Sam make the cert call as part of building your practice.

## RacketPro, for the record

You'll also encounter **RacketPro (RPO)** — a coach-education library, and the original source of [the Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) that Coach Up inherits through its seed material and credits. RacketPro is education, not a certification body in the PPR/IPTPA sense; Coach Up's relationship to it is simply: we adapted a good framework from it, and we say so.

## The one-line version

> A certification is content plus insurance. The apprenticeship is the craft. Get the cert for the coverage — Coach Up will help you pick it and pass it — and build the actual coaching here. They're two halves, not two options.
