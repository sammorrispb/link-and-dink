# The Readiness Bar

*"Are you ready to coach on your own?" is the question the whole apprenticeship answers. The Readiness Bar is that question made concrete — so graduation is a clear standard, not a mysterious judgment call.*

---

## Why the bar is written down

A readiness standard you can't see is a standard you can't work toward. The bar is public, specific, and the same for every apprentice — so that across all twelve weeks you know exactly what you're climbing toward, and so that [graduation](../flows/graduation-flow) is never a surprise.

## The bar — three things, all true

An apprentice clears the Readiness Bar when **all three** of these are true:

### 1. The self-assessment pattern holds

A **consistent five-of-seven or better** on [The Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment), across the back half of the cohort — not one good week, a sustained pattern. And **box 6 — "every player can name what they worked on" — is reliably hit**, because that's the box that separates teaching from activity. And **box 7 — the honest debrief — is non-negotiable**: it either happened or it didn't.

### 2. Sam can say yes to three plain statements

The mentor who watched twelve weeks of reps can honestly answer yes to all three:

- *"I'd send my own players to this coach."*
- *"They read a room and adjusted, more than once, without being told."*
- *"They handle the adult conversations — the awkward player, the fit talk — professionally."*

### 3. The capstone holds up

The [final observed solo event in Week 12](../curriculum/week-12) is a clean, real demonstration of the coach the apprentice has become — the [Pillars](../frameworks/the-coach-up-pillars) held, the room read, the [debrief](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) led honestly afterward.

## No surprises

The bar is checked continuously, not sprung at the end. Every [weekly 1:1](../flows/weekly-1-1-flow) is implicitly measuring against it; [Week 11's readiness check-in](../curriculum/week-11) makes it explicit while there's still a week to close a gap. By [Week 12](../curriculum/week-12), the three-yes sign-off just confirms what everyone already knew.

## If the bar isn't cleared

The apprenticeship extends — a defined runway, one named focus area. **We don't fail for slow.** A coach who's growing but not yet at the bar gets more reps and a clear target, not a door. The only genuine non-clear is a coach who isn't growing despite honest feedback and real support — and that's rare, and it's a conversation Sam owns directly. (Full mechanics: [The Graduation Flow](../flows/graduation-flow).)

> Three things, all true: a held self-assessment pattern, Sam's three plain yeses, and a capstone event that holds up. Written down, checked all along, no surprises at the end.
