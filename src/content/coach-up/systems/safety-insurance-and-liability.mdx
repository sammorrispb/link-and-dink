# Safety, Insurance & Liability

*The least exciting system in the handbook, and a non-negotiable one. A coach who isn't thinking about safety is one bad afternoon away from harming a player and themselves.*

---

## Keep players safe

- **Warm-ups are not optional.** [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids), shift #7: most of your players are coaching toward *lifelong play*, and lifelong play depends on not getting hurt. Build a real warm-up into every session — the warm-up rally in [the session arc](../frameworks/session-design-framework) is a start, but for older bodies, build more.
- **Coach the adult body in front of you.** Adults bring old injuries, less elastic tissue, and real consequences for overdoing it. Watch for the player pushing past their limit, especially the competitive one who won't say they're hurting. Load is real — manage it.
- **Heat and cold are safety issues.** On a hot MoCo afternoon, hydration breaks and shade aren't comfort niceties — they're the job. Know the signs of heat illness. Adjust or cancel; a session is never worth a player's health.
- **Know the basics.** Where's the nearest help. What's your plan if someone goes down. You don't need to be a medic — you need to not be caught flat-footed. Carry a basic first-aid kit.

## Cover yourself

- **Liability insurance matters.** Once you're running real sessions, you need to be insured. This is the single most concrete reason Coach Up *recommends* certification — see below.
- **Understand what you're responsible for.** When you run a session, players' safety is partly in your hands. That's a real responsibility; treat it like one. Waivers, where appropriate, are part of operating professionally — know your venue's requirements.

## Where certification comes in

This is the practical reason for the certification recommendation. Coach Up's position, in full at [Where Coach Up Meets Certification](../progression/where-coach-up-meets-certification):

- A certification (PPR, IPTPA) is **content plus a path to insurance.** It does not make you a better coach — the apprenticeship does that. But it gets you *insured*, and that matters the moment you're running real events.
- **Coach Up recommends you get certified once you're running real sessions** — and does *not* subsidize the cost. You pay for it; we help you pick the right pathway and study what matters.
- Get the cert for the coverage. Get the craft here.

## Common failures

- **Skipping warm-ups** because the session feels casual.
- **Coaching the player you wish you had** instead of the actual adult body in front of you — and missing the injury risk.
- **Treating heat as a comfort issue** instead of a safety one.
- **Running real, paid sessions uninsured** because certification felt like a someday problem.

> Safety isn't a vibe, it's a system. Warm them up, watch the adult body, respect the heat — and get insured before you're running real sessions, not after.
