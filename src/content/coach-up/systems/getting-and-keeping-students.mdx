# Getting & Keeping Students

*Coaching skill alone doesn't make a coaching practice. A great coach with an empty calendar is a hobbyist. This is the system that fills the calendar — and, more importantly, keeps it full.*

---

## The funnel — getting students

Your formats are a funnel, widest at the front:

```
SOCIAL          Widest. Lowest commitment. Often someone's first
                touch — built for belonging, not skill. The front door.

PICKUP / LADDER  Recurring play. A reason to come back, a community
                forming, your name attached to a good night.

CLINIC          A themed, promotable on-ramp to real instruction.

SMALL GROUP /    The core teaching relationship. The deepest
1:1 LESSON       commitment, the highest value, the through-line.
```

- **The [social](run-a-social) is the front door.** It's the easiest yes for a stranger. A great social sends players deeper into the funnel.
- **The [pickup](run-a-pickup) and [ladder](run-a-ladder) build the community** that makes everything else easier — recurring players who know your name and trust your room.
- **Word of mouth is the strongest channel, and it's earned, not bought.** Every well-run session is marketing. The player who had a great clinic tells two friends. ([The post-event debrief](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) — running every session well — *is* part of acquisition.)
- **Move people deliberately.** Don't wait for players to find the next step — invite them. "You'd get a lot out of the third-shot clinic" is a kindness and a sale at once.

## Retention — keeping students

Acquisition gets attention; retention is where the practice is actually won or lost. It's cheaper, more durable, and mostly comes down to discipline.

- **The close is a retention act.** Every session ends with a clear takeaway and a felt sense of progress ([the session arc](../frameworks/session-design-framework)). A player who can't name what they got won't be sure it was worth it.
- **The [running focus](../vocabulary/term-running-focus) is the through-line that makes players stay.** A player who feels *developed* — part of an arc, not a series of disconnected sessions — has a reason to keep coming. A player who feels *processed* doesn't.
- **Remember the adult's real reasons.** [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids): they came partly for the people, and they're chasing lifelong play, not a podium. Design belonging into your sessions and protect their bodies, and you're coaching to what actually keeps them.
- **The retention scorecard isn't the rating — it's *do they still play, and do they still love it.*** That's the LTAD "Active for Life" standard, and it's the one that matters. A coach who moved a player up a rating but burned them out failed. A coach whose players are still on the court, still happy, a year later — succeeded.

## Common failures

- **All acquisition, no retention** — constantly chasing new players because the old ones quietly drift off.
- **No funnel** — running disconnected events with no path from a first social to a lasting lesson relationship.
- **Treating players as transactions** — no running focus, no through-line, no felt progress.
- **Coaching the rating instead of the love of the game** — winning the metric, losing the player.

> Acquisition is a funnel — social at the front, the lesson relationship at the core. Retention is a discipline — the close, the running focus, and coaching to the reasons adults actually keep playing.
