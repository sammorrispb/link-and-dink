# Court & Logistics

*Nobody gets into coaching for the logistics. But a great session on a court you didn't actually secure is a great session that didn't happen. This is the unglamorous backbone.*

---

## Courts

- **In the Coach Up apprenticeship, court booking for events is largely handled for you** — that's part of the deal, and part of how the [rev-share](../vocabulary/term-rev-share) is structured. Your job early on is to *run* the room, not source it.
- **But understand the landscape**, because you'll own it eventually. In Montgomery County the spectrum runs from free public courts (most MoCo parks — no court cost, first-come dynamics) to low-cost outdoor paid courts to premium indoor facilities. Each tier changes the economics of an event and what you can charge.
- **For your own lessons and clinics, you may be sourcing the court yourself.** Know your options, know the booking process, and confirm — a "probably available" court is not a booked court.
- **Always have a backup.** Public courts especially can be taken, wet, or closed. Know your fallback before you need it.

## Equipment

- **Bring more than you think you need.** Balls — extras, because they crack and roll away. A few loaner paddles for newcomers and socials. Targets and cones for [constraint games](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit). A way to keep score visible for events.
- **Check it before you leave.** A clinic with too few balls is a clinic running at half speed.

## Weather

- **Outdoor pickleball lives and dies on weather.** Have a clear policy and communicate it before it's relevant: what conditions cancel, how players are notified, what happens to fees.
- **Decide early and tell people clearly.** A late, vague cancellation costs you trust. An early, clear one — even a disappointing one — keeps it.
- **Heat is a safety issue, not just a comfort one** — see [Safety, Insurance & Liability](safety-insurance-and-liability).

## Player communication

- **Confirm before, follow up after.** Players should never wonder whether a session is on, where it is, or what to bring. A clear confirmation message before, a brief thank-you-and-next-time after.
- **Be the reliable signal.** Times always in Eastern, location specific, instructions clear. Your communication *is* part of the product — sloppy logistics comms read as a sloppy coach.
- **Build the contact habit.** Every event is also a list of people you can invite to the next thing. Capturing and respecting that contact info is part of [Getting & Keeping Students](getting-and-keeping-students).

## Common failures

- **Assuming a court instead of confirming it.**
- **No weather policy** — or one communicated too late.
- **Under-packing equipment.**
- **Vague or missing player communication** — the silent session-killer.

> Logistics is the boring half of the job and it's not optional. Confirm the court, over-pack the bag, have a weather policy, and never leave a player guessing.
