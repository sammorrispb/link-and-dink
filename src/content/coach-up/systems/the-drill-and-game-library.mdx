# The Drill & Game Library

*Raw material, not a recipe book. Every entry here is a starting point you'll adapt with [the Constraint Design Toolkit](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit). The skill is never "run the game" — it's "turn the dials so this game reps what* this *group needs today."*

---

## How to use this library

Organized by which block of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression) each game reps. For every game: what it reps, the setup, and the dials you'll most often turn. Pull a game when you're [designing a session](../frameworks/session-design-framework) — then make it yours. A game you can't say the one-sentence purpose of doesn't belong in your session.

---

## Soft game & dinking

**Cross-Court Dink Rally.** *Reps:* dink control, patience. *Setup:* two players, cross-court dinks only, count the rally. *Dials:* shrink the target to half the kitchen (harder); add "must move them" (no two dinks to the same spot).

**Dink to a Target.** *Reps:* dink placement. *Setup:* cones or spots in the kitchen; points for hitting the target. *Dials:* target size, cooperative vs. competitive scoring.

**Seven-Eleven (kitchen game).** *Reps:* the soft game under score pressure. *Setup:* a dink-only game, first to a number. *Dials:* allow speed-ups only off a high ball (adds the read); count required before anyone can attack.

**Mirror Dinking.** *Reps:* patience, defense, footwork. *Setup:* one player feeds the pattern, the other must match it. *Dials:* swap roles; add lateral movement.

## Reset & transition

**Drop-and-Reset.** *Reps:* the reset from pressure. *Setup:* coach or partner feeds attackable balls at a player at mid-court; the player must reset to the kitchen. *Dials:* feed pace, feed predictability (vary it — [repetition without repetition](../vocabulary/term-repetition-without-repetition)).

**Run the Transition.** *Reps:* resetting on the move. *Setup:* player starts at the baseline, must advance to the kitchen line through fed balls at their feet. *Dials:* number of balls, pace, add a "must stop and split to hit" rule.

**Survive the Firefight.** *Reps:* block volley, knowing when to reset. *Setup:* feed a hard ball at a player at the line; they block-reset it down. *Dials:* feed pace; progress to a live exchange with a "reset the low ball" rule.

## Third shot

**Drop-or-Drive Decision.** *Reps:* the third-shot decision ([Hierarchy](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) Level 1). *Setup:* live third shots; before the ball, the player must call "drop" or "drive." *Dials:* coach controls the return depth so the *right* answer changes; require the call out loud.

**Third Shot Gauntlet.** *Reps:* third shot under repetition. *Setup:* serving team must land a playable third before the point "counts." *Dials:* target size; fed vs. live return; cross-court only.

## Serve & return

**Deep Return Targets.** *Reps:* return depth. *Setup:* a deep zone behind the service line; points for landing returns in it. *Dials:* zone depth; add "then advance to the line" to train the move-off.

**Serve Consistency Ladder.** *Reps:* serve reliability. *Setup:* player must land a streak of serves in; miss resets the streak. *Dials:* streak length; add a depth target.

## Net play & speed-ups

**Block-or-Punch.** *Reps:* the volley read. *Setup:* feed a mix of hard and attackable balls; player must block the hard one, punch the soft one. *Dials:* mix ratio; feed unpredictability.

**Earn the Speed-Up.** *Reps:* speed-up *selection*. *Setup:* a dink game where a speed-up is only legal off a ball above net height. *Dials:* tighten the legal-attack window; add "at a body only."

**Hands Battle Boxes.** *Reps:* fast-hands exchanges + the reset exit. *Setup:* a fast volley exchange at the line; whoever drops a ball low must reset, not battle. *Dials:* starting distance; add targets.

## Whole-game & point construction

**Constraint Games.** *Reps:* whatever you choose. *Setup:* a normal game with one rule added — "every third must be a drop," "no speed-up before the fourth ball," "win the point with a dink and it's worth two." *Dials:* this *is* the dial — the constraint is the lesson.

**Call Your Shot.** *Reps:* intent and target selection. *Setup:* live points; players must name their target before key shots. *Dials:* which shots require a call; cooperative vs. competitive.

**Mixed-Level Handicap Game.** *Reps:* competitive play across a level spread. *Setup:* a normal game with a built-in handicap — stronger side starts down, or can only score off a designated shot. *Dials:* the handicap itself (see [The Mixed-Level Playbook](../frameworks/the-mixed-level-playbook)).

## The rule that governs all of it

None of these is a finished product. Each one is a frame you turn the [dials](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit) on until it reps what *this* group needs *today* — and each one needs your one-sentence answer to "what is this repping?" before it goes in a session. The library gives you raw material; [game-based teaching](../vocabulary/term-game-based-teaching) and the Toolkit tell you what to do with it.

> These are starting points, not scripts. Pull a game, turn the dials, make it rep the thing — and never run one you can't name the purpose of.
