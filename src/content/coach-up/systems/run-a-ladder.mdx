# How to Run a Ladder

*A ladder is the long game of event formats. A pickup is a night; a ladder is a season. It's the format that turns one-time players into a recurring community — which makes it a retention engine, not just an event.*

---

## What a ladder is

An ongoing, ranked competition. Players hold a position on a "ladder"; they play matches over a set period — a few weeks, a season — and results move them up or down. There's a standing, a story, and a reason to come back next week to defend or climb.

The format varies — challenge-based (you challenge the player above you), session-based (you play a set each week and points accumulate) — but the core is the same: **persistent stakes over time.**

## What makes it work

- **A clear, simple scoring system.** Players must be able to understand, at a glance, where they stand and how they move. If the standings need explaining, the ladder is too complicated. Simple and visible beats clever.
- **Visible standings.** The ladder only works if players can *see* it — an up-to-date standing they can check between sessions. The standing is the product.
- **A reliable cadence.** A ladder lives on rhythm. Same night, same place, every week. Inconsistency kills it faster than anything — a player can't climb a ladder that isn't there.
- **A defined season with an end.** Open-ended ladders go stale. A start, an end, a champion, a reset — the finish line is what creates urgency, and the reset is what invites everyone back in.

## The decisions you own

- **The format and scoring** — chosen for *this* group's size and level spread, and kept simple.
- **The cadence** — set it, protect it, never let it slip. You are the reason it's reliable.
- **Keeping the standings live** — update them promptly and publicly after every session. Stale standings read as a coach who stopped caring.
- **Managing the social temperature** — ranked competition over weeks can breed friction. Same job as [Pot Night](run-a-pot-night): competitive *and* warm, and you set the tone.

## Common failures

- **Overcomplicated scoring.** If you can't explain the standings in two sentences, simplify.
- **Letting the cadence slip.** One skipped or rescheduled week and the momentum is gone.
- **Stale standings.** The ladder *is* the standings — if they're not current, there's no ladder.
- **No end point.** A ladder with no season has no urgency and no fresh start.

## Where it fits

The ladder is your strongest [retention](getting-and-keeping-students) tool in the event toolkit — it manufactures a reason to come back that a one-off pickup can't. Lower coaching load than a lesson, higher commitment than a pickup, and run over weeks it builds exactly the recurring community that makes a coaching practice sustainable.

> A ladder is a season, not a night. Keep the scoring simple, the standings live, and the cadence sacred — that rhythm is the whole product.
