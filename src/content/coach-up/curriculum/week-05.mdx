# Week 5 — Your First 1:1 Lesson

*Week four was running a room. Week five is teaching a person. They are different skills, and the 1:1 is where the diagnostic craft you've been building gets its purest test.*

---

## The theme

An event is breadth — many players, light coaching, the room. A 1:1 is depth — one player, your full attention, the [Hierarchy](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) running the entire time. Week five is your first real rep at depth: a genuine intake, a genuine diagnosis, and the start of a developmental through-line for one student.

## Your reps

- **Do a real intake.** Before the lesson, a proper [intake conversation](../systems/intake-and-rating-literacy) — the why, the history, the constraints, their self-assessment. Hold their stated focus as a hypothesis, not a finding.
- **Run a full 1:1, observed.** Design it with [the Session Design Framework](../frameworks/session-design-framework), run [the 1:1 structure](../systems/structure-a-1-1-lesson), Sam observing under the silent-observer rule. The warm-up read tests your intake hypothesis; the session teaches the focus you actually found.
- **Resist the over-coaching trap.** One player in front of you makes it tempting to narrate everything. Don't. Even one-on-one, the player has to *do* far more than they *hear*.
- **Set the running focus.** Close the lesson with one clear takeaway, and write down the student's [running focus](../vocabulary/term-running-focus) — the through-line you'd pull next time. This is the rep that turns "a lesson" into "developing a player."

## The 1:1

Week five's 1:1 with Sam debriefs your first lesson — but the sharpest question will be diagnostic honesty: *did you teach the focus the player asked for, or the one your warm-up read actually found?* Sam will also pressure-test your running-focus note — does it name something real and specific, or is it vague?

## What it teaches

- [How to Structure a 1:1 Lesson](../systems/structure-a-1-1-lesson) — the format, run for real.
- [Intake & Rating Literacy](../systems/intake-and-rating-literacy) — the conversation before the lesson.
- [Running Focus](../vocabulary/term-running-focus) — the through-line that makes development possible.
- [The Session Design Framework](../frameworks/session-design-framework) — the design process, applied to one player.

## The standard

By the end of week five you've run a real private lesson, you led with a genuine diagnosis rather than just the student's request, and you left with a written running focus that names something specific. The lesson doesn't have to be polished. It does have to be *real teaching of a real person* — and it has to have a through-line you could pick up next time.

> Week five: from running a room to teaching a person. A real intake, a real diagnosis, a running focus written down. Depth, not breadth — and the diagnostic craft gets its purest test.
