# Week 4 — Run an Event Solo

*Phase 2 — DO IT — opens here. Three weeks of foundation, and now the work is yours. You run a full event, solo, and Sam watches without saying a word.*

---

## The theme

This is the week the apprenticeship stops being preparation and becomes the thing itself. You've shadowed, co-run, learned the Pillars and the Hierarchy, drafted a plan. Week four you put it all on a court alone — and discover, in the only way anyone ever discovers it, what you actually know.

## Your reps

- **Design and run a full event solo.** Almost always [a pickup](../systems/run-a-pickup). You own all of it — the design, the logistics, the opening, the rotation, the energy, the close.
- **Hold the silent-observer setup.** Sam is there, observing, and will *not* interrupt — not to correct, not to rescue — barring a genuine safety issue. The players experience you as the coach. The full runbook is [The First-Event Flow](../flows/first-event-flow); week four is when it fires.
- **Read the room and adjust.** Something will go sideways — a lopsided game, an awkward number. [Reading the Room](../frameworks/reading-the-room) and turning a dial mid-event is the skill being tested.
- **Lead your own debrief.** Within ten minutes, you run [the Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) on yourself — *before* Sam says anything. You diagnose your own event first.

## The 1:1

Week four's 1:1 is built around the solo event. You bring your self-led debrief; Sam, who watched silently precisely so this conversation could be rich, goes second — questions first, then one note. The silent-observer rule and the coach-led debrief are two halves of one design: your rep, your diagnosis, mentorship layered on top.

## What it teaches

- [How to Run a Pickup](../systems/run-a-pickup) — now executed solo, not co-run.
- [The First-Event Flow](../flows/first-event-flow) — the before/during/after runbook for exactly this moment.
- [The Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) — led by you, on your own event.
- [Reading the Room](../frameworks/reading-the-room) — the live-adjustment skill the solo event tests hardest.

## The standard

The standard for week four is *not* a flawless event. It's a real event, run solo, honestly debriefed — and at least one moment where you read the room and adjusted without being told. Imperfect is expected and correct; the gap between what you planned and what happened is exactly where the next eleven debriefs do their work.

> Week four: you run a full event alone, Sam watches in silence, and you lead your own debrief. Phase 2 has begun — the work is yours now.
