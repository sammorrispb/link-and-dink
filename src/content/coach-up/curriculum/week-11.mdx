# Week 11 — A Full Autonomous Week + Peer Observation

*Week eleven is the dress rehearsal. You run a full week as if the apprenticeship were already over — no observer, no scaffolding — and you start the honest conversation about whether you're ready.*

---

## The theme

Every week so far had Sam observing, co-designing, or debriefing close. Week eleven takes the scaffolding away on purpose. You run your own week autonomously — the way a graduated coach runs theirs — so that the only remaining question for week twelve is the formal one. And you do something new: you watch a cohort-mate coach, which turns out to be one of the fastest ways to sharpen your own eye.

## Your reps

- **Run a full autonomous week.** Your own sessions — events, lessons, whatever your week holds — designed, run, and debriefed by you, with no observer in the room. The [self-assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) and [debrief](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) habits carry you; they're the structure now that the scaffolding is gone.
- **Observe a cohort-mate.** Watch one of your two cohort-mates run a session, with a structured lens — what's one thing they do well that you want to steal, one pattern worth discussing, how they handled one tricky moment. Observing with no responsibility is the fastest way to see clearly.
- **Run your funnel.** An autonomous week is also a [Getting & Keeping Students](../systems/getting-and-keeping-students) week — you're not just running sessions, you're running the practice you built in week ten.

## The 1:1

Week eleven's 1:1 is the **readiness check-in**. You and Sam look honestly at where you stand against [The Readiness Bar](../progression/the-readiness-bar) — the autonomous week is the evidence, the self-assessment pattern is the record. This is deliberately *before* week twelve: graduation should hold no surprises, and this 1:1 is where any remaining gap gets named while there's still a week to work on it.

## What it teaches

- [The Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) — now the only structure, with no observer to lean on.
- [The Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) — the pattern across the autonomous week is the readiness evidence.
- [Getting & Keeping Students](../systems/getting-and-keeping-students) — the practice, run autonomously.
- [The Readiness Bar](../progression/the-readiness-bar) — what "ready to graduate" actually means.

## The standard

By the end of week eleven you've run a full week the way a graduated coach runs one — autonomously, on your own habits — and you've had the honest readiness conversation with Sam. If the check-in surfaced a gap, you have week twelve to close it. The point of week eleven is that nothing in week twelve should be news.

> Week eleven: the dress rehearsal. A full autonomous week on your own habits, a cohort-mate observed, and an honest readiness check-in — so graduation holds no surprises.
