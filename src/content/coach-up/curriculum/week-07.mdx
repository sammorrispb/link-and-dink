# Week 7 — Small Groups & the Mixed-Level Reality

*A 1:1 is depth. An event is breadth. The small-group lesson is both at once — and it's where the Pillars get genuinely hard. Week seven is the toughest test of the apprenticeship so far.*

---

## The theme

Most adult coaching actually happens in small groups, and a small group of adults is almost never one level. Week seven puts the two hardest realities together: holding [both Pillars](../frameworks/the-coach-up-pillars) across three or four players *simultaneously*, while those players span a real level spread. This is the week the craft has to integrate.

## Your reps

- **Run a small-group lesson.** [The small-group format](../systems/run-a-small-group-lesson) — the standard session arc, delivered to three or four players at once. The Pillars stop being easy here: you have to *design* for Active Court (no lines) and *spread* Feedback Density (everyone, not just the interesting one).
- **Apply the Mixed-Level Playbook.** [The Mixed-Level Playbook](../frameworks/the-mixed-level-playbook) — mixed level is the default, not the emergency. The core move: change the player, not the game — personal constraints inside one shared game, so the strong player isn't bored and the new one isn't exposed.
- **Coach the adult, per player.** [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids) — four different adults, four different whys, four different bodies and egos. You can't coach to the average.
- **Track a running focus for each player.** Harder than in a 1:1, just as essential. A note per player, every session.

## The 1:1

Week seven's 1:1 works mixed-level scenarios hard — the bored strong player, the small quiet weak player, the bridge pairing, the handicap. Sam will also check the thing small-group coaches most often drop: *did your feedback actually reach all four players, or did one or two get coached half as much?*

## What it teaches

- [How to Run a Small-Group Lesson](../systems/run-a-small-group-lesson) — the core format of most adult coaching.
- [The Mixed-Level Playbook](../frameworks/the-mixed-level-playbook) — making a level spread work inside one game.
- [The Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression) — what to teach, and where each player sits on it.
- [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids) — four adults, four sets of reasons.

## The standard

By the end of week seven you can hold both Pillars across a small group, you can run a genuinely mixed-level session where every player was challenged and nobody was exposed, and you have a running-focus note for each of them. This is the hardest week of Phase 2 — if it felt like a stretch, that's the week working.

> Week seven: depth and breadth at once. Both Pillars across four players, a real level spread handled by changing the player not the game, a through-line on every one of them. The craft has to integrate here.
