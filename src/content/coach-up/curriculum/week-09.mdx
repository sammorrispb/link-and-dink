# Week 9 — Run Your Clinic, and the First Look at Pricing

*Week nine closes Phase 2. You run the clinic you designed last week — the biggest, most public format in your toolkit — and you start the turn toward Phase 3 by looking, for the first time, at coaching as a business.*

---

## The theme

Week nine has two halves. The first is the capstone of the "DO IT" phase: running the clinic you designed in week eight, at scale, in public, holding the Pillars across a big room. The second is the bridge into "OWN IT": the first honest conversation about what your coaching is *worth* and how you'd price it.

## Your reps

- **Run your clinic.** The clinic you and Sam designed in [week eight](week-08). [Hold the Pillars at scale](../systems/design-a-clinic) — stations and self-sustaining games for Active Court, a deliberate circuit of the room for Feedback Density. Deliver exactly the theme the flyer promised.
- **Debrief it against the promise.** Your [post-event debrief](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow), plus one clinic-specific question: *did every player leave with what the theme promised them?*
- **Take a first pass at pricing.** Begin [Pricing & Selling](../systems/pricing-and-selling) — and start a draft [rate card](../vocabulary/term-rate-card). This is a first look, not a finished business plan; week ten goes deep. The job this week is to get the uncomfortable first numbers on paper.

## The 1:1

Week nine's 1:1 debriefs the clinic — the biggest single thing you've run — and then opens the pricing conversation. Expect Sam to push on the most common new-coach instinct: pricing low out of nerves. The 1:1 plants the principles ([price the value not the hour; discounts train clients to expect discounts](../systems/pricing-and-selling)) that week ten builds into a real plan.

## What it teaches

- [How to Design a Clinic](../systems/design-a-clinic) — now run, not just designed.
- [Pricing & Selling](../systems/pricing-and-selling) — the first pass.
- [Rate Card](../vocabulary/term-rate-card) — the artifact you start drafting this week.
- [The Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow) — applied to your biggest event.

## The standard

By the end of week nine you've run a clinic that delivered its theme, you've debriefed it honestly, and you have a draft rate card with real — if uncomfortable — numbers on it. Phase 2 is complete: you've now run every format the apprenticeship covers. Phase 3 is about making it a practice you own.
