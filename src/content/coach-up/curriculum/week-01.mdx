# Week 1 — Philosophy Before Reps

*You can't run what you haven't seen, and you can't see well without knowing what you're looking at. Week one is philosophy and observation — on purpose, before a single solo rep.*

---

## The theme

The temptation in week one is to want a paddle and a court immediately. Coach Up says no — not yet. A week one spent absorbing the *why* and watching the *what* is what makes weeks two through twelve compound instead of flail. You're building the lens before you pick up the tool.

## Your reps

This week the "reps" are observation reps:

- **The philosophy-transfer session.** One-on-one with Sam, early in the week — a real conversation walking [the Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) and the *why* underneath the whole system. This is the most important hour of the cohort; come ready to think, not just listen.
- **Shadowing.** You watch Sam or a senior coach run real sessions. Before each one, they narrate the plan — the focus, the players, the design. After, you debrief together: *what did you see, what surprised you?*
- **The reading.** The full [Creed layer](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) of the handbook. Not skimmed — read. [The Coach as Environment Designer](../creed/the-coach-as-environment-designer) and [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations) especially; they're the load-bearing ideas.

## The 1:1

Week one's 1:1 *is* the philosophy-transfer session — the deepest, most foundational conversation of the apprenticeship. It ends with Sam setting you **your one thing to watch for**: a single lens to carry into your observation and your first reps. Everyone's is different. Yours is the start of your personal through-line.

## What it teaches

- [The Coach Up Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) — the ten beliefs that drive every decision.
- [The Coach as Environment Designer](../creed/the-coach-as-environment-designer) — the central metaphor of the whole system.
- [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations) — why the method is what it is, with sources.
- [The Apply-to-Onboard Flow](../flows/apply-to-onboard-flow) — how you got here, and what week one is inside that pipeline.

## The standard

By the end of week one you should be able to say, in your own words, what "we coach the conditions, the rally teaches" actually means — and you should have a watched-it-happen example to attach to it. You don't need to be able to *do* it yet. You need to be able to *see* it.

> Week one is the lens, not the tool. Transfer the philosophy, train the eye, leave with your one thing to watch for. The reps start next week — on a foundation.
