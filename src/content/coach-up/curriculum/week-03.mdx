# Week 3 — Diagnosis: Reading a Player

*Week two put your hands on the work. Week three trains the most important skill underneath it: reading a player and knowing what to actually fix.*

---

## The theme

A coach who can run a room but can't diagnose a player is a host, not a coach. Week three is the diagnostic week — the [Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) becomes your default lens, and you start practicing the discipline of finding the cause before you teach the symptom. It's the last foundation week before the solo reps begin.

## Your reps

- **Learn the Hierarchy.** [The Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) — Intent, Outcome, Technique, Tactics, walked in order. This is the framework the rest of the apprenticeship leans on hardest. Practice the on-yourself drills in it.
- **Co-run with the diagnostic lens on.** A second co-run, but now your job inside it is different — you're watching players *through* the Hierarchy, practicing [error detection](../vocabulary/term-error-detection) in real time even as you help run the room.
- **Learn the Feedback Framework.** [The Feedback Framework](../frameworks/the-feedback-framework) — once you've diagnosed *what* to fix, this is *how to say it* so it lands.
- **Draft your first lesson plan.** A real session design, written on the index card from the Session Design Framework. It doesn't have to be run yet — it has to be *drafted*, so Sam can review it with you.

## The 1:1

Week three's 1:1 does two things. First, Sam walks the Hierarchy *with* you on real players you both observed — "did she know her intent?" not "her stroke looked wrong." Second, Sam runs [the Lesson-Plan Review Flow](../flows/lesson-plan-review-flow) on your first draft plan — the six questions, questions-not-answers, so you learn to catch your own weak design.

## What it teaches

- [The Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) — the diagnostic spine of the whole system.
- [The Feedback Framework](../frameworks/the-feedback-framework) — how to deliver what the diagnosis found.
- [Error Detection](../vocabulary/term-error-detection) — the skill underneath diagnosis.
- [The Lesson-Plan Review Flow](../flows/lesson-plan-review-flow) — pressure-testing a design before it meets a court.

## The standard

By the end of week three you can walk the Hierarchy in order without skipping to Technique, you've practiced reading players through it live, and you have a drafted lesson plan that survived the six review questions. You're not a polished diagnostician yet — that takes the whole cohort and beyond — but the lens is on and you know you're meant to diagnose before you teach.

> Week three: the Hierarchy becomes your default lens, you practice reading players through it, and you draft and pressure-test your first real lesson plan. The foundation is set — solo reps start next week.
