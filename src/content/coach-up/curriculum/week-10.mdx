# Week 10 — The Business of Coaching

*Phase 3 — OWN IT — opens here. You can coach. Week ten is about turning "can coach" into "runs a coaching practice" — the part a course or a certification never teaches.*

---

## The theme

A great coach with an empty calendar is a hobbyist. Week ten is the business week: the rate card, the rev-share, the funnel, the cert decision. None of it is the craft of coaching — and all of it is what decides whether the craft becomes a livelihood.

## Your reps

- **Finish your rate card.** Take the week-nine draft to a finished [rate card](../vocabulary/term-rate-card) — your written menu of 1:1s, small groups, clinics, packages. Priced on the [Pricing & Selling](../systems/pricing-and-selling) principles: value not hour, hold the number, no nerves discount.
- **Understand the rev-share.** [Rev-Share](../vocabulary/term-rev-share) — L&D shares only on events where it covers the court; your lessons and clinics are 100% yours. Know how this shapes what you charge for an event versus a lesson.
- **Map your funnel.** [Getting & Keeping Students](../systems/getting-and-keeping-students) — social at the front, the lesson relationship at the core. Map *your* version: how a stranger becomes a lasting student, and the retention discipline that keeps them.
- **Make the cert decision.** With Sam's input, decide your [certification](../progression/where-coach-up-meets-certification) pathway — which one, what level, when. Get certified for the insurance; you've already built the craft here.

## The 1:1

Week ten's 1:1 is a business planning session. Sam works your rate card, your funnel, and your cert decision with you — and pushes on the honest questions: is the rate card brave enough, is the funnel real or wishful, is the cert timing right. You leave with a business plan, not just a coaching skill.

## What it teaches

- [Pricing & Selling](../systems/pricing-and-selling) — finished, this week, into a real rate card.
- [Getting & Keeping Students](../systems/getting-and-keeping-students) — the acquisition funnel and the retention discipline.
- [Rev-Share](../vocabulary/term-rev-share) — the economics of the apprenticeship and beyond.
- [Where Coach Up Meets Certification](../progression/where-coach-up-meets-certification) — the cert decision, made.

## The standard

By the end of week ten you have a finished rate card you'd actually quote, a mapped funnel you could actually run, and a certification decision you've actually made. The craft was Phase 2's job. Week ten's job is the practice the craft lives inside.

> Week ten: build the business. A finished rate card, the rev-share understood, a real funnel mapped, the cert decision made. "Can coach" becomes "runs a coaching practice."
