# The 12-Week Roadmap

*The apprenticeship promises you'll know what week 5 looks like before week 1 starts. This is that promise, kept. Twelve weeks, three phases, a clear arc — and a set of constants that never change.*

---

## The shape

Twelve weeks, three phases. Each phase is a different relationship to the work.

```
PHASE 1 — SEE IT        Weeks 1–3    foundation
  You watch, you absorb the philosophy, you take your
  first reps with Sam right there. Reps start in week 2.

PHASE 2 — DO IT         Weeks 4–9    reps
  You run the work — events solo, 1:1s, small groups,
  a clinic you designed. Sam observes and debriefs.

PHASE 3 — OWN IT        Weeks 10–12  ownership
  You run autonomously, build the business, and graduate
  — or extend with a named focus.
```

## Phase 1 — SEE IT (Weeks 1–3)

You can't run what you haven't seen. Phase 1 transfers the philosophy and gets your eye trained before the real reps begin.

- **[Week 1](week-01)** — Philosophy before reps. Sam's philosophy transfer; shadowing; your "one thing to watch for."
- **[Week 2](week-02)** — The Pillars, and your first reps. Co-run a pickup; your first paid reps.
- **[Week 3](week-03)** — Diagnosis. The Hierarchy of Assessment; co-run again; draft your first lesson plan.

## Phase 2 — DO IT (Weeks 4–9)

The heart of the apprenticeship. You run the work; Sam watches silently and debriefs hard.

- **[Week 4](week-04)** — Run an event solo.
- **[Week 5](week-05)** — Your first 1:1 lesson.
- **[Week 6](week-06)** — Constraint design and game-based teaching.
- **[Week 7](week-07)** — Small groups and the mixed-level reality.
- **[Week 8](week-08)** — Design a clinic.
- **[Week 9](week-09)** — Run your clinic, and the first look at pricing.

## Phase 3 — OWN IT (Weeks 10–12)

You stop being supervised and start being a coach.

- **[Week 10](week-10)** — The business of coaching.
- **[Week 11](week-11)** — A full autonomous week, plus peer observation.
- **[Week 12](week-12)** — Graduation.

## The constants — what's true in every week

The phases change. These never do. They run through all twelve weeks, and they're the actual habits of the craft:

- **The post-session self-assessment, every session.** Seven boxes, ten minutes, honest. ([The Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment).)
- **The post-event debrief, every session.** You lead it, immediate, one note. ([The Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow).)
- **The weekly 1:1, never skipped.** Rescheduled if it must be, never dropped. ([The Weekly 1:1 Flow](../flows/weekly-1-1-flow).)
- **The 24-hour answer guarantee.** Any question to Sam gets a real reply inside a day.
- **The cohort group thread.** Three apprentices, one channel — shared wins, shared problems, accountability.
- **One coaching note per debrief.** On your players and on yourself. ([The One-Note Rule](../vocabulary/term-one-note-rule).)

## How graduation works

Twelve weeks is the *shape*, not a guarantee. Graduation is earned through the three-yes sign-off — yours, Sam's, and the pattern's — at [Week 12](week-12). Short of that, the apprenticeship extends with one named focus, because **we don't fail for slow.** The full mechanics are in [The Graduation Flow](../flows/graduation-flow), and graduation moves you onto [The Coach Up Ladder](../progression/the-coach-up-ladder).

> Three phases — see it, do it, own it. Reps from week two. Constants in every week. Graduation earned, not timed. That's the whole map.
