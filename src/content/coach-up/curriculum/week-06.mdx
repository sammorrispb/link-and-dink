# Week 6 — Constraint Design & Game-Based Teaching

*By now you can run a room and teach a person. Week six sharpens the thing underneath both — designing the environment so the game does the teaching, and you don't have to.*

---

## The theme

[The Creed's first belief](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) is "the environment teaches, we design it." Week six is where that belief becomes a practiced skill. You stop thinking of a session as "drills I run" and start thinking of it as "constraints I set" — and you discover how much teaching happens when you set them well and then get quiet.

## Your reps

- **Design a constraint-game session.** A full session whose skill blocks are [constraint games](../vocabulary/term-constraint-led-coaching), not isolated drills. Every block has a one-sentence answer to "what does this rep?"
- **Run it, turning dials live.** [The Constraint Design Toolkit](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit) — the three dials, one turn at a time, off-ramp in your pocket. The rep here is *adjusting the design in real time* to keep the struggle productive.
- **Use the library as raw material.** Pull from [The Drill & Game Library](../systems/the-drill-and-game-library) — and adapt every game to your group. Running a library game blind, without making it yours, is the thing this week trains you out of.
- **Practice getting quiet.** A well-set constraint is already teaching. Week six is where you practice talking less and trusting the design more.

## The 1:1

Week six's 1:1 reviews your constraint designs — Sam runs the "what does this block rep?" question on each one, and works with you on the dial you most need: usually either *setting constraints that make the skill the easy path* (not just a required one) or *reading when to turn a dial mid-session*. This is craft week; the 1:1 goes deep on craft.

## What it teaches

- [The Constraint Design Toolkit](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit) — the three dials and the rules for turning them.
- [Constraint-Led Coaching](../vocabulary/term-constraint-led-coaching) — the method at the core of the system.
- [Game-Based Teaching](../vocabulary/term-game-based-teaching) — games as the default vehicle, drills as the exception.
- [The Drill & Game Library](../systems/the-drill-and-game-library) — raw material to adapt, never a script to run blind.

## The standard

By the end of week six you can design a session as a sequence of deliberate constraint games, you can turn a dial mid-session when the room tells you to, and — the real tell — you talked *less* this week than last week, because the games were carrying more of the load. Designing the environment is the hardest craft skill and the most central one; week six is where it stops being a concept and starts being a habit.

> Week six: the core craft sharpens. Design with constraints, turn the dials live, adapt the library — and get quiet, because a good design is already teaching.
