# The Apply-to-Onboard Flow

*How a prospective coach becomes an apprentice. It's a short pipeline, and every step is built to make the apprenticeship a* fit *— for both sides — before anyone commits.*

---

## When it fires

From the moment someone submits a Coach Up application through the end of their first week in the cohort.

## The flow

### Step 1 — Apply

The prospective coach submits an application — who they are, how they play, why they want to coach, their availability and commitment. It takes a few minutes. It's the start of a conversation, not a test.

### Step 2 — Review, within five days

Every application gets a real response **within five days** — where they stand, clearly. That five-day promise is itself part of the brand: Coach Up answers fast and answers straight. A fit, a not-yet, or a "let's talk" — but never silence.

### Step 3 — The fit conversation

For promising applicants, a real conversation with Sam. It runs both directions:
- **Sam is assessing fit** — not "are you already a great player," but "are you coachable, will you do the reps, do you want the craft." The [Right for you / Not for you](../creed/what-coach-up-is-not) lines are the honest filter.
- **The applicant is assessing fit too.** This is an apprenticeship, not a job offer — they should be deciding whether Coach Up is what they want as much as Sam is deciding whether they're a fit.

### Step 4 — The offer and cohort placement

A fit gets an offer into a cohort. Cohorts are **three coaches** — small on purpose, because three is what keeps the weekly 1:1 honest and the mentorship real. A fit who can't join the current cohort is held for the next; the small size is a real constraint, not manufactured scarcity.

### Step 5 — Week One: the philosophy transfer

Onboarding is the first week of [the 12-week roadmap](../curriculum/week-01), and it's owned by Sam directly:
- **A dedicated philosophy-transfer session** — Sam, one-on-one, walking the new apprentice through [the Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) and the *why* underneath the whole system. This happens early in week one.
- **Shadowing** — the apprentice watches Sam (or a senior coach) run real sessions, with narration before and a debrief after. Reading "the environment teaches" is a rule; *watching* it run is the "oh, that's what they meant" moment.
- **The one thing to watch for** — week one ends with Sam setting the apprentice a single thing to focus their eye on as the reps begin.

## The principles

- **Fit is mutual.** Both sides are deciding. An apprenticeship that isn't a real fit helps no one.
- **Fast and honest.** Five days, a clear answer, the real version. The hiring pipeline runs in the same voice as the coaching.
- **Small cohorts are load-bearing.** Three is a design decision — it's what makes the rest of the apprenticeship possible. (See [The Cohort Charter](../progression/the-cohort-charter).)
- **Week one is philosophy before reps.** You shadow and absorb the *why* before you run anything. The reps start in week two — on a foundation, not a blank slate.

> Apply, hear back in five days, have an honest two-way fit conversation, get placed in a cohort of three — then week one is Sam transferring the philosophy and you shadowing the real thing. Reps start week two.
