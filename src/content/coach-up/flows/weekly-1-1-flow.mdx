# The Weekly 1:1 Flow

*This is the part of Coach Up that a course, a certification, or a job board cannot sell you. One hour a week, with a mentor who watched closely enough to tell you the truth. It is the spine of the apprenticeship.*

---

## When it fires

**Once a week, every week, for all twelve weeks.** In person when possible, video when not. It is *never skipped* — if a week's slot can't happen as planned, it's rescheduled, not dropped. The reliability of this hour is a promise Coach Up makes; protecting it is a discipline the apprentice shares.

## The flow

The apprentice comes prepared — this is your hour, and you lead it.

### Step 1 — The week in review (about 15 min)

Walk Sam through the week's reps — the events, lessons, or clinics you ran. Lead with your [post-event debriefs](post-event-debrief-flow): what worked, what you'd change, the patterns you're noticing. You're not reporting; you're *thinking out loud with a mentor*.

### Step 2 — The pattern (about 15 min)

This is the heart of it. Look at your [self-assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) results across the week. A box missed once is noise; the *same* box missed three sessions running is a signal — and that signal is this conversation. Sam's job here is to help you see the pattern and name the one thing to work on. Your job is to be coachable about it.

### Step 3 — The look-ahead (about 15 min)

Next week's reps. Sam may [review a lesson plan](lesson-plan-review-flow) with you, talk through an event you're nervous about, or work a specific scenario — the awkward player, the mixed-level clinic, the parent-style conversation with an adult client.

### Step 4 — The open lane (about 15 min)

Whatever you need. Business questions — pricing, students, the [rate card](../vocabulary/term-rate-card). The cert decision. A hard moment from the week. Something about the craft you can't get from the handbook. This is unstructured on purpose; the apprenticeship is wide, and this is where the edges get covered.

## The principles

- **The apprentice prepares and leads.** Show up with your debriefs, your patterns, your questions. An unprepared 1:1 wastes the most valuable hour of your week.
- **Honest, both directions.** Sam tells you the true thing — in the moment, not saved for a review. You bring the real version of your week, not the flattering one.
- **It's mentorship, not management.** This isn't a status meeting. It's [the thing Coach Up actually is](../creed/what-coach-up-is-not) — and it only works if you treat it that way.
- **It feeds the Readiness Bar.** The patterns surfaced here, week over week, are exactly what [the Readiness Bar](../progression/the-readiness-bar) is measuring at graduation. Nothing in the 1:1 is a surprise later.

> One hour a week, never skipped. You prepare it, you lead it: the week in review, the pattern, the look-ahead, the open lane. This is the apprenticeship — protect it.
