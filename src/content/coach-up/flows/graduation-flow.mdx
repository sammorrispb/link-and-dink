# The Graduation Flow

*Week twelve. The apprenticeship doesn't end with a certificate in the mail — it ends with a clear, honest answer to one question: are you ready to coach on your own?*

---

## When it fires

**Week twelve** — the final week of the cohort. See [Week 12](../curriculum/week-12) for how the week itself is structured; this flow is the graduation *decision* inside it.

## The flow

### Step 1 — The final observed solo event

The apprentice runs a full event, solo, with Sam observing under the same silent-observer rule as [the first event](first-event-flow) — but now it's a capstone, not a first attempt. It's a clean look at the coach the apprentice has become over twelve weeks.

### Step 2 — The full rubric self-score

The apprentice scores themselves against [The Coach Competency Rubric](../progression/the-coach-competency-rubric) — honestly. This is [the Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) habit scaled up to the whole apprenticeship: where am I strong, where am I still thin, what's my honest read on my own readiness.

### Step 3 — The three-yes sign-off

Graduation to Coach tier requires three yeses — and they have to line up:

1. **The apprentice's yes** — the honest self-score says ready.
2. **Sam's yes** — the mentor who watched twelve weeks of reps can say, plainly, "I'd send my own players to this coach."
3. **The pattern's yes** — the body of evidence agrees: consistent five-of-seven on [the Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) across the back half of the cohort, the [Readiness Bar](../progression/the-readiness-bar) cleared, the [debriefs](post-event-debrief-flow) showing real growth.

A graduation is all three saying yes about the same coach. One yes alone isn't graduation.

### Step 4a — Graduate to Coach

Three yeses: the apprentice graduates and moves onto [the Coach Up ladder](../progression/the-coach-up-ladder) at the **Coach** tier — running their own events, lessons, and clinics, with the apprenticeship's scaffolding now behind them and the mentorship relationship continuing in a lighter form.

### Step 4b — Extend, with a named focus

Not three yeses yet? Then the apprenticeship **extends** — a defined number of additional weeks with *one named focus area*. This is the seed system's principle, carried straight over: **we don't fail for slow.** A coach who's growing but not yet there gets more runway and a clear target, not a door. The only real failure is a coach who isn't growing despite honest feedback and support — and that's a conversation Sam owns directly, and it's rare.

## The principles

- **Graduation is earned, not timed.** Twelve weeks is the shape, not the guarantee. The three yeses decide it, not the calendar.
- **No surprises.** Nothing at graduation should be new information. The [weekly 1:1s](weekly-1-1-flow) surfaced every pattern along the way; week twelve just makes the call official.
- **An extension is support, not punishment.** A named focus and more reps is the growth-mindset move. The handbook itself says it: better than yesterday, and the timeline bends to the coach.
- **The relationship doesn't end.** Graduation moves the apprentice onto the ladder — it changes the form of the mentorship, it doesn't end it.

> Week twelve: a final observed event, an honest full-rubric self-score, and a three-yes sign-off — apprentice, Sam, and the pattern all agreeing. Three yeses graduate you to Coach. Short of that, you extend with a named focus — because we don't fail for slow.
