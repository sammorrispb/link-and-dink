# The Post-Event Debrief Flow

*The single most important habit in the apprenticeship. [The Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) says reps beat theory — but only reps* with feedback. *This flow is that feedback loop, run on yourself, every time.*

---

## When it fires

**Within ten minutes of finishing** any session, event, lesson, or clinic you ran. Courtside or just off it. Not that night, not tomorrow — the memory is freshest now, and a debrief done cold is a debrief done soft.

## The flow

### Step 1 — Run the Self-Assessment (about 2 minutes)

Walk [The Apprentice Self-Assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment) — the seven boxes. Which did you hit? Which didn't you? Be honest; the flattering version is worthless. Pay special attention to box 6 — *could every player name what they worked on?* — it's the one that catches "busy but didn't teach."

### Step 2 — Name the one thing that worked (about 1 minute)

Not a list. *The* one thing. The best decision you made, the moment you read the room right, the constraint that landed. Naming what worked matters as much as naming what didn't — it's how you keep the things you're already doing well instead of drifting off them.

### Step 3 — Name the one thing you'd do differently (about 1 minute)

Again — *one*. The [one-note rule](../vocabulary/term-one-note-rule) applies to debriefing yourself just as hard as it applies to coaching a player. Pick the single biggest lever. If you try to fix five things, none of them change.

### Step 4 — Update your running focus notes (about 2 minutes)

For a lesson or recurring group: note each player's [running focus](../vocabulary/term-running-focus) and any movement on it. For an event: note anything about specific players worth remembering. This is the step new coaches skip, and it's the step that turns a series of sessions into actual development.

### Step 5 — Name what you need before next time (about 1 minute)

The forward-looking close. A resource, a script, a question for Sam, a constraint you want to design better, a rep you want to repeat. This is the bridge into [the weekly 1:1](weekly-1-1-flow) — recurring needs and recurring missed boxes are exactly its agenda.

## The principles

- **Immediate.** Within ten minutes. Cold debriefs go soft.
- **Honest.** The real version. Self-assessment box 7 — "did I run an honest debrief?" — is the integrity check on this whole flow.
- **One note.** One thing that worked, one thing to change. Not an inventory.
- **You lead it.** This is a coach-led debrief on yourself. Self-diagnosis beats top-down correction — and it builds the habit that keeps you growing long after the apprenticeship ends.

## Why it's the keystone habit

Reps without this flow are just hours — unexamined, non-compounding. Reps *with* it compound: every session sharpens the next, every missed box becomes a learned pattern, every "one thing I'd do differently" is a small upgrade you actually keep. A coach who debriefs honestly every time gets better whether or not anyone is watching. That's the entire point of [the Creed's belief #10](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) — the coachable coach wins.

> Within ten minutes, honest, one note. Seven boxes, what worked, what to change, the running focus, what you need next. The rep doesn't count until you've debriefed it.
