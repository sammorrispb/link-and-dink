# The Coach Up Handbook

*The full coaching system behind the apprenticeship. If you read nothing else, read this page.*

---

## What this is

Coach Up is Link & Dink's paid apprenticeship for new pickleball coaches. Twelve weeks, a cohort of three, real reps from week one. This handbook is the curriculum behind it — the thing you're actually here to learn.

It exists because the alternative is what most new coaches get: a weekend certification, a job board, and a basket of balls. That produces people who can recite the rules of pickleball. It does not produce coaches. Coaching is a craft, and a craft is learned the way crafts have always been learned — by doing the work next to someone who already does it well, and getting honest, specific feedback every time.

**This handbook is not a substitute for the reps.** It's the shared language and the mental models that make the reps add up. Read it, then go run a session, then come back.

## Who it's for

You, the apprentice — first, and most. Also Sam, as the reference he coaches against, so the apprenticeship stays consistent from one cohort to the next. And eventually the coaches who graduate and mentor the next cohort. One system, written down, so it can be taught instead of re-improvised.

## The one-breath version

> **We coach the conditions. The rally teaches. The coach who learns fastest wins.**

You are not the entertainment. You are not the talker. You are not the rally partner. You design the environment, protect the rally, read the players, and say the one thing that matters — then you get out of the way and let the game do the teaching. And you do all of that while staying the most coachable person on the court, because the apprenticeship only works if you out-learn everyone around you.

That's the whole philosophy. The rest of this handbook is how.

## The six layers

The handbook is built in six layers. Each one answers a different question.

| Layer | The question it answers | Where |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Creed & Philosophy** | *Why do we coach this way? What drives the call?* | `creed/` |
| **Frameworks** | *What mental models do I run a session on?* | `frameworks/` |
| **Vocabulary** | *What words do we all use the same way?* | `vocabulary/` |
| **Systems** | *How do I actually run an event, a lesson, a clinic?* | `systems/` |
| **Flows** | *What do I do when X happens?* | `flows/` |
| **The 12-Week Roadmap** | *What does week 5 look like before I start?* | `curriculum/` |
| **The Progression Model** | *What's the path past graduation?* | `progression/` |

Read them roughly in that order. The Creed sets the why; everything downstream cites back to it. The Roadmap is written last and on purpose — it threads every other layer into the twelve weeks, so each week points at the exact frameworks, systems, and terms it teaches.

## How to use it

**Apprentice, week one?** Read this page, then the [Coach Up Creed](creed/the-coach-up-creed), then the [Roadmap Overview](curriculum/roadmap-overview). That's your first hour.

**Running a session this week?** Pull the relevant system from `systems/` and the framework it leans on. Don't read the whole handbook before every session — read the one piece the session needs.

**Stuck on a player?** Go to [The Hierarchy of Assessment](frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment). It's the diagnostic spine — what to fix, and in what order.

**Want the research behind it?** [Research Foundations](creed/research-foundations) names every source. Nothing in this handbook is invented; it's adapted from how the best academies in the world train athletes and coaches, then translated to a pickleball court in Montgomery County.

## A standing reminder

This handbook will change. As cohorts move through it, the debriefs sharpen the frameworks, the systems pick up edge cases, and the roadmap adapts. That's not drift — that's the system working as designed. The day this handbook stops changing is the day Coach Up stopped learning.
