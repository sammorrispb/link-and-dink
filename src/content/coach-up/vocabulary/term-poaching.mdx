# Poaching

*An aggressive intercept — done well it ends points, done badly it opens the court wide.*

**Definition.** When a player moves laterally into their partner's area of the court to intercept a ball — typically a volley — that the partner would otherwise have played. The poacher takes the ball early and aggressively, usually to end or seize control of the point.

**Why it matters.** A well-timed poach pressures opponents who get comfortable hitting to one player, and it converts a neutral ball into an attack. But a poach also vacates the poacher's own side — if it doesn't end the point, the team is briefly out of position.

**Coaching it (adults).** Two coaching truths. First, a poach needs **communication** — a call, a signal, or a clear pre-point plan — or the poacher and partner end up in the same place with a hole next to them. Second, poach for a *reason* (a read, a known weak ball), not for a thrill. Coach it as a deliberate tactic, tied to who covers the [middle seam](term-middle-seam).

> Poach with a plan and a call — surprise the opponent, not your partner.
