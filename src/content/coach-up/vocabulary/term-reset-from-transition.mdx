# Reset From Transition

*The [reset](term-reset), performed in the hardest place to perform it — on the move, through no-man's-land.*

**Definition.** A soft, absorbing shot played while a team is advancing through the [transition zone](term-transition-zone) toward the kitchen line, taking the pace off a ball hit at the player's feet and dropping it softly into the opponents' kitchen, so the advance can continue.

**Why it matters.** A team that hits a third-shot drop still has to *cross* the transition zone — and the opponents are firing balls at their feet the whole way. The reset from transition is the skill that lets a team survive that crossing. Without it, every advance gets driven back; with it, the team gets to the line.

**Coaching it (adults).** This is the hard combination — a soft skill executed on the move, under pressure. Coach the sequence: a brief *controlled stop and split* to hit, soft hands to absorb, then continue forward. The classic adult errors are hitting on the run (no control) or stopping for good (camping in no-man's-land). It's an advanced layer on blocks 3 and 6 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression).

> Stop, soft, drop — absorb the ball, then keep climbing.
