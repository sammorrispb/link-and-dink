# Around the Post

*A shot that surprises people because it looks illegal and isn't — and because most players don't know to look for it.*

**Definition.** A shot that travels around the outside of the net post rather than over the net, landing in the opponents' court. It is fully legal, and — because it goes around, not over — it may legally travel *below* the height of the net.

**Why it matters.** The around-the-post (ATP) is the right answer to a ball that pulls a player well wide of the sideline — often a sharply angled cross-court dink. In that situation the ATP is frequently an outright winner, because opponents rarely cover for it. Knowing the shot *exists as an option* is half the skill.

**Coaching it (adults).** Most adults don't realize the ATP is legal, and even when they do, they instinctively try to lift the ball back *over* the net. The coaching point is mostly **recognition**: build the read — "when they pull you wide off the sideline, look around the post, not over the net." It's an advanced, situational shot; teach the recognition first, the execution second.

> Wide ball, go around — it's legal below the net, and it's a winner.
