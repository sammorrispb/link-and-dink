# Constraint-Led Coaching

*The methodological core of Coach Up. If you understand this term, you understand why the handbook is built the way it is.*

**Definition.** A coaching method in which the coach sets a *constraint* — a rule, target, condition, or limit on play — so that the desired skill becomes the natural solution to the problem in front of the player. The player discovers the skill through play instead of being instructed in it directly.

**Why it matters.** It's the mechanism behind the [Creed's first belief](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) — "the environment teaches, we design it." Skills learned through constraints tend to transfer to real play better than skills drilled in isolation, because they were learned *in* a game-like context to begin with.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** This is a methodology term — it's about *your* practice, not a shot. The full operational version is [The Constraint Design Toolkit](../frameworks/the-constraint-design-toolkit): the three dials and the rules for turning them. The research lineage (Newell's constraints model; the ecological-dynamics coaching tradition) is in [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations).

> The game teaches. You just set the rules.
