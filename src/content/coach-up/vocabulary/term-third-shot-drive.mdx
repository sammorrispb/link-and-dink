# Third Shot Drive

*The other third shot. Not the "power" option to the drop's "skill" option — a different tactical tool for a different read.*

**Definition.** A hard, relatively flat third shot driven low at the opponents — ideally at their feet or hips — intended to force a weak, poppy reply that the driving team can then attack or convert into a drop on the fifth shot.

**Why it matters.** The drive is the right call when the opponents' net position is imperfect, when the return was short enough to step into, or when a player's drop simply isn't reliable that day. The key coaching truth: **a drive is rarely a point-ender — it's a point-shaper.** It's shot three of a sequence, not the finish.

**Coaching it (adults).** Adults love the drive because it feels powerful and decisive. The error is treating it as a winner — swinging for an ace and either missing or hitting an attackable ball back. Coach the *intent*: the drive's job is to produce a weak ball, not to end the rally. Pair it explicitly with the drop in a decision drill so the player practices *choosing*, not just swinging. The decision drive-or-drop is [Hierarchy](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) Level 1 work.

> Low and at their feet — you're setting up the fifth, not ending the point.
