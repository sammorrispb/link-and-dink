# Transition Zone

*The most dangerous ground on the court — and the ground every point asks you to cross.*

**Definition.** The mid-court region between the baseline and the kitchen line, often called "no-man's-land." It's the area a team passes through while advancing from the back of the court to the kitchen line, typically behind a third shot drop.

**Why it matters.** Balls land at a player's feet in the transition zone — the hardest ball to handle. A player who *camps* there gets picked apart; a player who *moves through* it, resetting any tough balls along the way, gets to the kitchen line where the point is actually contested. The zone isn't a position; it's a passage.

**Coaching it (adults).** Two coaching points. First, **keep moving** — adults often stop in the transition zone to hit, then stop again, in a series of frozen snapshots. Coach a controlled, continuous advance. Second, **reset, don't drive, from here** — see [reset from transition](term-reset-from-transition). Block 6 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression).

> Move through it, don't live in it — reset and keep climbing.
