# Third Shot Drop

*The most pivotal shot in pickleball — and the one most adult players get wrong at the Intent level before they ever get it wrong at the Technique level.*

**Definition.** A soft, arcing shot played as the serving team's third shot, designed to land in the opponent's kitchen. It forces the opponents to hit up, neutralizing the advantage they hold by being at the net first, and buys the serving team time to advance to the kitchen line.

**Why it matters.** The serving team starts every point at a positional disadvantage — back at the baseline, opponents at the net. The third shot drop is the primary tool for erasing that disadvantage. A team that can execute it reliably can win points on serve; a team that can't is stuck driving and hoping.

**Coaching it (adults).** Run [the Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) hard here. Most third-shot-drop failures are **Intent** problems — the player never decided "drop" before the ball came, so the body half-committed. Confirm the decision first. Only then coach technique, and coach it externally: "apex the ball over the net," "land it soft in the kitchen" — not "bend your knees." Block 5 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression).

> Lift with the legs, not the wrist. Apex it over the net.
