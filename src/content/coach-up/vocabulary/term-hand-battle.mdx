# Hand Battle

*The fast, chaotic exchange that breaks out after a speed-up. Exciting to watch, and the last thing to teach.*

**Definition.** A rapid back-and-forth of volleys between players at or near the kitchen line, usually triggered by a [speed-up](term-speed-up). It's won by reaction speed, a compact paddle position, accurate placement at bodies, and — critically — knowing when to *stop* battling and reset.

**Why it matters.** Hand battles decide a lot of points at the intermediate-and-up level. But they're high-variance: a player who battles every ball, including the ones below net height, loses as many as they win. The skill isn't just fast hands — it's fast hands *plus* the judgment to fold a losing exchange into a [block volley](term-block-volley) or reset.

**Coaching it (adults).** This is block 7 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression) and it's taught **last** for a reason — it's the most chaotic part of the game, and a player needs the base, the soft game, and the block volley already working or it's just a coin flip. Coach the paddle position (out front, compact) and the *exit* (reset the low ball) as much as the speed.

> Paddle out front, compact and quick — and reset the second it drops low.
