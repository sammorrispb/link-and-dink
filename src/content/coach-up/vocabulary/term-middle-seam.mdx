# Middle Seam

*The space between partners — the most attacked real estate in doubles, and the most miscommunicated.*

**Definition.** The area down the middle of the court between two doubles partners. It's both an *offensive target* — a ball hit into the seam can freeze two players each thinking the other has it — and a *defensive responsibility* that partners must sort out, usually in advance.

**Why it matters.** "The middle solves the riddle" is a cliché because it's true — attacking the seam produces hesitation, collisions, and balls left for nobody. Conversely, a team that has clearly decided *who takes the middle* removes their opponents' best easy target.

**Coaching it (adults).** This is a **communication** lesson disguised as a positioning lesson. The default rule worth teaching: the player with the forehand in the middle takes it, or whoever called it takes it — but the real coaching point is that partners must *talk and decide*, not assume. Run drills that specifically attack the seam so partners are forced to sort it out under live pressure.

> The middle is won by the team that calls it — talk, don't assume.
