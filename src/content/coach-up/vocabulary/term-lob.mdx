# Lob

*A shot that's a great tactic and a terrible habit, depending entirely on whether it was a decision.*

**Definition.** A high, arcing shot lifted over opponents positioned at the kitchen line, intended to land deep in their court and force them to retreat and play a defensive ball — or to scramble for an overhead.

**Why it matters.** A well-chosen lob resets a point a team was losing at the net, pushes aggressive opponents off the line, and exploits opponents who don't move well backward. A poorly chosen lob — short, or hit when opponents are already deep — hands over an easy overhead and the point.

**Coaching it (adults).** The lob is the classic *panic shot* — adults reach for it when they're under pressure and out of ideas, and a panic lob is almost always too short. Coach the **read**: lob when opponents are tight to the line and you have time, not when you're scrambling. Pair it with teaching the [overhead](term-overhead) so players understand both sides of the exchange. A lob is [Hierarchy](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) Level 4 — usually a tactics decision, not a technique problem.

> Lob the read, not the panic — over the shoulder, deep, on purpose.
