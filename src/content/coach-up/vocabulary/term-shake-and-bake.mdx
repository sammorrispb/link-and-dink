# Shake and Bake

*A two-person play with a name as fun as the move. One partner drives, the other pounces.*

**Definition.** A coordinated doubles tactic: one partner hits a hard third-shot drive ("the shake"), and the other partner immediately crashes forward toward the net to volley away the expected weak, popped-up reply ("the bake").

**Why it matters.** It's a planned, aggressive alternative to the third-shot-drop-and-advance pattern. Against opponents who struggle to handle pace at the net, the shake-and-bake turns a drive into a near-automatic point — the driver doesn't need a winner, just a ball that produces a pop-up for the crasher.

**Coaching it (adults).** This is a *team* skill — it only works with commitment and timing from both players, so coach it as a pair, not as two individuals. The crasher's common error is leaving too early or too late; the driver's is trying to hit a winner instead of a ball that *forces a pop-up*. Introduce it only once partners can already communicate — it's an intermediate, plan-it-in-advance play.

> Driver and crasher — one hits hard, one runs in, both committed.
