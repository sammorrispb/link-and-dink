# Erne

*A flashy, advanced play — a player relocating around the kitchen to ambush a ball at the net.*

**Definition.** A play in which a player moves around the outside of the non-volley zone — by jumping or stepping around the kitchen's sideline edge — to volley a ball very close to the net. It's legal because the player's feet are outside the kitchen entirely when they volley; they never volley *from inside* it.

**Why it matters.** The erne lets a player attack a cross-court dink or a predictable shot early and from an unexpected angle, often for a clean winner. It also functions as a *threat* — opponents who know a player can erne start second-guessing their dinks.

**Coaching it (adults).** This is a genuinely advanced, situational play — it should come well after the fundamentals and only for players who want it. Coach the **legality** clearly first (feet outside the kitchen, not stepping in), then the *read* (a predictable, attackable cross-court ball), then the footwork. For most adult rec players, awareness that the erne exists matters more than drilling the execution.

> Around the kitchen, not in it — take the dink early and end it.
