# The Debrief

*The single most important habit in the apprenticeship. Reps without it are just hours.*

**Definition.** The brief, structured, honest self-review a coach conducts within about ten minutes of finishing a session — what worked, the one thing to do differently, what they need before next time. In Coach Up it's coach-led: you run your own debrief, on yourself.

**Why it matters.** [Creed belief #6](../creed/the-coach-up-creed) — reps beat theory, *but only reps with feedback*. The debrief is that feedback loop. A coach who runs honest debriefs compounds every session into the next; a coach who skips them just accumulates unexamined hours. It's also the integrity check on everything else — the [self-assessment](../frameworks/the-apprentice-self-assessment)'s box 7 is "did I run an honest debrief, or the comfortable one?"

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** Three standards: **immediate** (within ten minutes, memory freshest), **honest** (the real version, not the flattering one), and **one note** (one lever, not a self-inventory). The full structured procedure — including how it runs after an event versus after a lesson — is [The Post-Event Debrief Flow](../flows/post-event-debrief-flow).

> Within ten minutes, honest, one note — the rep doesn't count until you've debriefed it.
