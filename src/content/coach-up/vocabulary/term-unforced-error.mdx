# Unforced Error

*The point a player gives away. Understanding this term reframes what "getting better" even means at the rec level.*

**Definition.** A point lost because a player missed a shot they were not under real pressure to miss — a dink into the net on a comfortable ball, a drive sailed long, a serve fault — as opposed to a *forced* error, where an opponent's good shot caused the miss.

**Why it matters.** At the rec level, the large majority of points are *lost*, not *won* — handed over via unforced error rather than taken by a winner. This is the most important strategic fact you can give an adult player: the fastest path to winning more is not hitting better shots, it's giving away fewer points. It reframes "improvement" away from flashy shotmaking and toward consistency and good decisions.

**Coaching it (adults).** Use it to redirect the adult who wants to learn winners: "the player across from you is going to hand you the point — your job is to still be in the rally when they do." It also sharpens diagnosis — an unforced error walked through [the Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) usually traces to no intent or a bad target, not a broken stroke.

> Most rec points are given, not won — cut the unforced errors first.
