# Target Selection

*Where to aim — a decision, made before the shot, that's coachable on its own.*

**Definition.** The tactical choice of *where* a shot is intended to go — at a body, to a corner, cross-court, into the seam, at the weaker opponent — as distinct from the technical question of whether the player can execute it.

**Why it matters.** Two players with the same strokes can be very different players, because one *picks targets* and the other just hits the ball back. Target selection is what turns shotmaking into point construction. It also clarifies coaching: a shot can fail because the target was wrong (a tactics problem) even when the execution was fine.

**Coaching it (adults).** Adults frequently have no target at all — they're returning the ball, not aiming it. This maps straight onto [the Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment): "what were you going for?" is an Intent *and* a target question. Coach target selection by *requiring a target* in constraint games ("every dink has to go cross-court," "find the body"), so choosing a target becomes a habit rather than an afterthought.

> Pick the target before the swing — where, then how.
