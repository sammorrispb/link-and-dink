# Rate Card

*The small business artifact that makes a coach look like a professional instead of a hobbyist.*

**Definition.** A coach's clear, written list of services and prices — private lessons, small-group sessions, clinics — including any package or recurring options. It's the reference a coach works from when a prospective student asks "how much?"

**Why it matters.** A coach without a rate card improvises a number every time someone asks, which reads as amateur, invites negotiation, and trains clients to expect discounts. A coach *with* one quotes the same confident number every time. It's also a planning tool — it forces the coach to actually decide what their time is worth.

**Coaching it (you, the apprentice).** Building your first rate card is a milestone in the [12-week roadmap](../curriculum/roadmap-overview) and a topic in [Pricing & Selling](../systems/pricing-and-selling). The common new-coach error is pricing the first lessons low out of nerves — don't; price fairly and over-deliver, because discounts are hard to walk back. Note Coach Up's structural point: you keep 100% of lesson and clinic revenue (see [rev-share](term-rev-share)) — the rate card is entirely yours.
