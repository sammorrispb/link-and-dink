# The Frame

*Two minutes of talking that earns the next fifty-eight minutes of doing.*

**Definition.** The brief opening segment of a session — typically a sentence or two, scripted in advance — in which the coach names the day's single focus and the *why* behind it: the problem this focus solves for the players.

**Why it matters.** Adults need the why before the how ([Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids), shift #1). The frame is where that why gets delivered. Done well, it converts a session from "drills the coach is making us do" into "the thing I'm here to fix" — which changes how hard a player works for the rest of the hour.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** Two rules. First, **script it** — write the one or two sentences in your [session plan](../frameworks/session-design-framework) so it lands tight, not rambling. Second, **the frame is the only monologue** — everything after it is players doing and you coaching alongside. If you're delivering long explanations during the skill blocks, the frame failed and you're trying to re-do it mid-session.

> Name the focus, sell the why, then stop talking and let them play.
