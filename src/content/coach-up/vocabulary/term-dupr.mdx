# DUPR

*A number every adult player will quote you. Useful — as long as you know exactly how much weight to put on it.*

**Definition.** DUPR (Dynamic Universal Pickleball Rating) is a widely adopted rating system that assigns players a numeric skill rating based on match results. It's become the common shorthand adult players use to describe their level.

**Why it matters.** A coach needs *rating literacy*: enough fluency to talk with players in the language they already use, to roughly group a [mixed-level session](../frameworks/the-mixed-level-playbook), and to set expectations. But a rating is a *blunt* instrument — two players at the same number can have completely different games, and a rating tells you nothing about *why* a player is stuck.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** Read the rating as a *starting hypothesis*, then let your eyes and [the Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) do the real assessment. A rating says roughly *where* a player is; only watching them play tells you *what they actually need*. Never let a number override what you're seeing on the court. More on using ratings well in [Intake & Rating Literacy](../systems/intake-and-rating-literacy).

> The rating gets you in the ballpark — your eyes do the actual assessment.
