# Andragogy

*The one-word reason this handbook has a whole document about not coaching adults like children.*

**Definition.** The study and practice of how *adults* learn — as distinct from *pedagogy*, the teaching of children. The concept is most associated with Malcolm Knowles, who identified the ways adult learners differ: they're self-directed, they bring a deep well of prior experience, they need to know *why* before they'll engage, they're problem-centered rather than subject-centered, and they're driven more by internal than external motivation.

**Why it matters.** Coach Up's curriculum is seeded from a youth academy, and a coach's instincts for "what coaching looks like" are usually formed in youth settings. Andragogy is the corrective: the *method* of good coaching is age-independent, but the *delivery* must shift for an adult.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** The full, practical translation — the seven concrete shifts — is in [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids). This term is just the name for *why that document exists*. See [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations).

> Adults aren't big kids — they need the why, they bring history, they want a say.
