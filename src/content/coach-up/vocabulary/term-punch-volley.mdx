# Punch Volley

*The offensive volley. Compact, firm, and aimed — not a swing, a punch.*

**Definition.** A firm volley played with a short, compact motion and minimal backswing, driven purposefully at an opponent's body, feet, or an open part of the court to apply pressure or win the point.

**Why it matters.** When a player gets an attackable ball at the net, the punch volley is how they convert it without overhitting. The defining feature is the *short motion* — the power comes from firmness and timing, not a big swing, which keeps the shot controlled and repeatable.

**Coaching it (adults).** Adults reach for a backswing whenever they want pace — and a backswing volley is late and wild. Coach **"short and firm, no backswing"** and give it a target. Pair it with the [block volley](term-block-volley): the player should be learning *block-or-punch* as a read (defend the hard ball, punch the attackable one), not just one or the other.

> Short and firm — punch through it, no backswing.
