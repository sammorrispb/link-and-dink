# Reset

*The survival shot. The thing that turns "I'm getting hammered" into "I'm still in this rally."*

**Definition.** A soft, controlled, defensive shot — played from mid-court, the transition zone, or under direct attack — that absorbs the pace of an incoming ball and drops it softly into the opponent's kitchen, neutralizing the attack and buying time to recover position.

**Why it matters.** Every player gets put under pressure. The reset is the difference between a player who folds the instant they're attacked and a player who can weather it, neutralize it, and keep the rally alive long enough to get back to neutral. A player who can reset is a player who can *survive* — and survival is what keeps a rally going long enough for anything else to be learned.

**Coaching it (adults).** The instinct under pressure is to swing harder or back up — both wrong. The reset is soft hands *into* the pressure: absorb, don't add. Coach the body to be still and low, the paddle out front, the contact soft. Teach it early — it's block 3 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression), right after the soft game, because it's the same soft hands applied under stress.

> Soft hands, quiet feet — just get it to land in their kitchen.
