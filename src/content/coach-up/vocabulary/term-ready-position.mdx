# Ready Position

*The posture a player returns to after every single shot. The home base of the body.*

**Definition.** The neutral athletic stance held between shots: feet roughly shoulder-width, knees soft, weight slightly forward on the balls of the feet, paddle up and out in front of the body around chest height, eyes up. From here a player can react to anything.

**Why it matters.** Pickleball at the kitchen line is fast. A player whose paddle drops to their side, or who stands upright between shots, simply cannot get the paddle to a quick ball in time. Ready position isn't a drill — it's the default the body must keep snapping back to, shot after shot, for a whole rally.

**Coaching it (adults).** The most common loss is the **paddle dropping** after a shot. Coach the *recovery*: "reset to ready" is the cue after every ball, not just at the start of the point. Adults from other paddle/racquet sports often have a lower, side-held ready position — pickleball's is higher and more in front.
