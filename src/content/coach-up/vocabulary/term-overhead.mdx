# Overhead

*The answer to a short lob — and a shot that lives or dies on footwork, not arm strength.*

**Definition.** A powerful shot struck from above head height, typically in response to a lob or a high floating ball, driven down into the opponents' court to win or seize the point.

**Why it matters.** The overhead is how a team punishes a poorly chosen lob. A team that can reliably put away short lobs takes the lob away from opponents entirely. A team that can't invites the lob all day.

**Coaching it (adults).** The overhead *feels* like an arm shot, so adults swing harder and harder and miss. The real skill is **footwork** — turning sideways and getting *behind* the ball early, so the swing itself can be controlled. Coach feet first: "the overhead is a footwork shot with a swing on the end." Also coach restraint — not every high ball is a kill; sometimes the controlled overhead back deep is the smarter play.

> Feet first, then the swing — get behind it and the shot is easy.
