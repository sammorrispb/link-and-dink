# The One-Note Rule

*The discipline that separates feedback that lands from feedback that turns to mush.*

**Definition.** The rule that a coach delivers *one* coaching note per teaching moment — one thing for the player to work on — rather than a list. The same rule applies to a coach debriefing themselves.

**Why it matters.** It's the most common new-coach feedback failure: a player misses a shot, the coach sees the grip *and* the footwork *and* the contact point *and* the shot choice, and says all four. None of them land. The player walks away with noise. One clear thing changes a player; four things change nothing.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** When you see four problems, [the Hierarchy of Assessment](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) picks the one — coach the lowest level that's actually broken, since fixing it often makes the others disappear. The other three are real and they'll still be there next rep. This applies just as hard to your own [debrief](term-the-debrief): one note on yourself, not a self-inventory. Full context in [The Feedback Framework](../frameworks/the-feedback-framework).

> One thing. The other three are real and they'll still be there next rep.
