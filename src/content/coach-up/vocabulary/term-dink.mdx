# Dink

*The spine of adult rec pickleball. The shot that wins more points than any drive ever will.*

**Definition.** A soft, controlled shot — hit off the bounce, usually from at or near the kitchen line — that arcs just over the net and lands in the opponent's non-volley zone, denying them an attackable ball.

**Why it matters.** At the rec level, points are won by patience and control far more than by power. The dink is how a player stays in a rally, moves an opponent around, and waits for a mistake or an attackable ball. A player who can dink with patience can compete; a player who can't will always be rushed into low-percentage shots.

**Coaching it (adults).** The universal new-adult error is *swinging* at the dink — treating it like a small groundstroke. Coach it as a *push* with soft hands and a quiet body. Use a target (the cross-court kitchen) so the player has an external focus, not "soft hands" alone. This is block 2 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression) — the soft game — and it pays back immediately, which makes it a confidence builder for nervous players.

> Soft hands, push don't swing — make them hit up.
