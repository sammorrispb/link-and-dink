# OPTIMAL Theory

*Three separate good ideas that the research says are even better when you do all three at once.*

**Definition.** A theory of motor learning from Gabriele Wulf and Rebecca Lewthwaite (2016) holding that learning is optimized by the *combination* of three factors: an **external focus** of attention, **enhanced expectancies** (the learner expecting to succeed — built through engineered success and positive feedback), and **autonomy support** (the learner having meaningful choice in the practice).

**Why it matters.** It ties together several threads of this handbook into one coherent target. It's not enough to use external cues *or* engineer success *or* support autonomy — the theory says stack all three and the effect compounds.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** Each factor has a home in the handbook: external cues in [the Hierarchy](../frameworks/hierarchy-of-assessment) and [external vs. internal cue](term-external-vs-internal-cue); [engineered success](term-engineered-success) in session design; autonomy support in [Coaching Adults vs. Kids](../creed/coaching-adults-vs-kids), where it's not just nice but a measurable learning aid. OPTIMAL theory is the reason to do all three deliberately. See [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations).
