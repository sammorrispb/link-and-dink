# Block Volley

*The defensive volley. The thing that keeps a player alive in a firefight at the line.*

**Definition.** A soft volley played against a hard-hit ball, in which the player absorbs the incoming pace rather than adding to it — a relaxed grip and a near-still paddle let the ball die and drop softly, ideally back into the kitchen.

**Why it matters.** When a player is attacked at the kitchen line, they have two options: speed it back (a [hand battle](term-hand-battle)) or take the pace off (a block). The block is the defensive choice — it neutralizes the attack and resets the point to neutral, the same job a [reset](term-reset) does, just executed out of the air.

**Coaching it (adults).** The instinct against a hard ball is to *grip tighter and punch* — exactly wrong. Coach a **loose grip and a quiet paddle**: "let their pace die on your strings." It's a feel skill, built with reps against progressively faster fed balls. Pair it with the [punch volley](term-punch-volley) so players learn block-or-punch as a choice.

> Loose grip, still paddle — let their pace die on your strings.
