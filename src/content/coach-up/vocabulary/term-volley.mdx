# Volley

*Taking the ball out of the air. The default at the kitchen line — and illegal the moment your feet are in the kitchen.*

**Definition.** Any shot hit before the ball bounces. Volleys are legal anywhere on the court *except* the non-volley zone — a player may not volley a ball while standing in, or touching, the kitchen.

**Why it matters.** At the kitchen line, the volley is the bread-and-butter shot — it takes time away from opponents and keeps the player in an attacking posture. Understanding *where* a volley is legal is also a rules fundamental that adults frequently get fuzzy on.

**Coaching it (adults).** The universal volley error is the big swing. Coach a compact motion: paddle out front, meet the ball early, "punch" rather than "swing." The volley family branches into the [block volley](term-block-volley), the [punch volley](term-punch-volley), and the [roll volley](term-roll-volley) — start with a clean, controlled neutral volley before layering those on.

> Paddle out front, meet it early — punch, don't swing.
