# Roll Volley

*An attacking volley for a ball you'd normally have to play safe — topspin is the trick that makes it work.*

**Definition.** A volley struck with a low-to-high brushing motion that imparts topspin on the ball. The topspin pulls the ball down into the court, allowing a player to attack a ball contacted slightly below net height that a flat punch would send long.

**Why it matters.** Without topspin, a ball below the net height has to be played soft — you can't drive it without it sailing. The roll volley extends a player's attack range: it converts "I have to dink this" balls into "I can pressure with this" balls. It's an advanced tool that meaningfully expands what a player can do at the line.

**Coaching it (adults).** This is genuinely advanced — don't introduce it until the [punch](term-punch-volley) and [block](term-block-volley) volleys are solid and the player has a real reason for it. The motion is a *brush*, low to high; the common error is a flat swipe with no spin. Coach the spin externally — "make the ball dive down" — and use a high target band (over the net but down fast) so the player can feel whether the spin happened.

> Low to high, brush up — topspin is what lets you attack a low ball.
