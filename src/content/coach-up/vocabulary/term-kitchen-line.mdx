# Kitchen Line

*The back edge of the kitchen, and the single most contested few feet of the court.*

**Definition.** The line marking the back boundary of the non-volley zone, 7 feet from the net. A player standing just behind it can volley legally while still being as close to the net as the rules allow.

**Why it matters.** The team that gets both players to the kitchen line, and holds it, controls the point — they take time away from opponents and dictate play. Nearly every tactical pattern in pickleball is about *getting to the line* (the third shot drop, the deep return) or *defending the line* (the dink rally, the reset). Position at the kitchen line is the prize the whole point is played for.

**Coaching it (adults).** Two habits to build: *getting there* (advancing off the deep return, advancing behind the third shot drop) and *holding there* (not backing up off a tough ball — resetting from the line instead). The instinct to retreat off pressure is the line-position killer; coach the reset as the alternative to the backpedal.

> Get there, hold it — the team at the line controls the point.
