# Speed-Up

*The decision to turn a soft rally into a fast one. The quality of the* decision *matters more than the quality of the* shot.

**Definition.** A deliberate attacking shot that suddenly adds pace to a slow exchange — typically driving a dink or soft ball at an opponent — to seize the initiative. A speed-up usually triggers a [hand battle](term-hand-battle).

**Why it matters.** Speed-ups are how soft rallies are broken open. But a speed-up is a *commitment*: it gives up the safety of the soft game and bets that the player can win the fast exchange that follows. A good speed-up is chosen; a bad one is a reflex.

**Coaching it (adults).** Adults speed up too early and off the wrong ball — a low ball, a ball they're off-balance for — because the soft game feels boring and they want action. Coach the **read**, not the shot: speed up off a *high* ball, *at a body*, when *you're balanced* — on your terms, not out of impatience. This is the front edge of block 7 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression), taught after the soft game is patient.

> Speed up the right ball — at a body, off a high one, on your terms.
