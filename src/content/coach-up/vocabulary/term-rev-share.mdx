# Rev-Share

*The economics of the apprenticeship, in one term. It's deliberately the cleanest split in coaching.*

**Definition.** Coach Up's revenue model. Link & Dink takes a share **only on events** — and only where L&D is paying for the court — on a scale that slides with the court cost. L&D takes **nothing** from a coach's private lessons or clinics. Those are entirely the coach's.

**Why it matters.** Most platforms take a cut of everything a coach teaches, regardless of what the platform contributed. Coach Up's model is the opposite: the share exists only where L&D is genuinely contributing (paying for a court, providing the event format and reach). It's an [apprenticeship-not-a-marketplace](../creed/what-coach-up-is-not) commitment expressed as math.

**Coaching it (you, the apprentice).** Understand the structure so you can plan your earnings: a free public court means you keep all of an event's fees; a premium indoor court L&D pays for means a larger share to L&D. Your [rate card](term-rate-card) — your lessons and clinics — sits entirely outside rev-share. Full detail in [Pricing & Selling](../systems/pricing-and-selling), and it shapes the path through the [Coach Up ladder](../progression/the-coach-up-ladder).
