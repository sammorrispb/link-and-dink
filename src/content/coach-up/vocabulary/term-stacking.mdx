# Stacking

*A positioning trick that lets a team keep their preferred sides regardless of the score.*

**Definition.** A tactic in which doubles partners position themselves on the same side of the court before the serve or return, then move to their intended positions as the ball is played — so that, for example, both players keep their forehands in the middle, or a stronger player always returns from a particular side.

**Why it matters.** Without stacking, the rotation of the serve forces players to alternate sides, which can put a player's weaker wing in a bad spot or split a team's strengths. Stacking lets a team *choose* their alignment. It's an intermediate tactic — useful, but not a day-one priority.

**Coaching it (adults).** Don't introduce this until the fundamentals are holding — it adds cognitive load, and a confused stack is worse than no stack. When you do teach it, keep it concrete: "you two want both forehands in the middle — here's how you line up to get there." Stacking depends on clear [stack-and-switch signals](term-stack-and-switch-signals) so partners don't collide or leave a side open.

> Stack to keep your strengths where they belong — forehands middle, best returner ready.
