# Deliberate Practice

*The reason "they've been playing for ten years" doesn't mean what people think it means.*

**Definition.** Practice that is *effortful*, has a *specific goal*, operates at the *edge of current ability*, and includes *immediate feedback*. It's distinct from mere repetition or accumulated playing time — hours alone don't build skill; deliberately structured hours do. The concept is most associated with the researcher K. Anders Ericsson.

**Why it matters.** It sets the standard for what a *rep* has to be to count. A player hitting a hundred comfortable, goalless dinks off a soft feed is logging activity, not practice. The same player with a target, a stretch goal, and fast feedback is doing the real thing.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** Coach Up's specific move is to *synthesize* deliberate practice with [constraint-led coaching](term-constraint-led-coaching) — the aim is game-like tasks that are also deliberate: focused, edge-of-ability, fed back fast. A well-designed constraint game *is* deliberate practice; a basket of aimless balls is not. See [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations).

> A rep with no goal and no feedback isn't practice — it's just activity.
