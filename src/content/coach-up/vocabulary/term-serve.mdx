# Serve

*Every point starts here. Which means consistency here is worth more than power here.*

**Definition.** The shot that puts the ball in play, struck underhand and below the waist, contacted in the air, and landed diagonally cross-court past the opponent's kitchen. A serve that lands short, wide, or in the net is a point given away before the rally starts.

**Why it matters.** A new player who double-faults serves — or floats short, attackable serves — is losing points and momentum for free. The serve doesn't need to be a weapon; it needs to be *reliable* and *deep*. A deep serve pins the returner back and buys the serving team a beat.

**Coaching it (adults).** Adults often arrive wanting a "big" serve. Redirect: the standard is **in, deep, repeatable.** Coach a simple, consistent motion and a target deep in the service box. Power and spin are later refinements, and only after the consistency is locked. Block 4 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression) — paired with the return, because together they're how every point begins.

> Deep and in, every time — the serve's job is to start the point, not win it.
