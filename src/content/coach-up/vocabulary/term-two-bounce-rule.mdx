# Two-Bounce Rule

*A rule that's really a strategy. It's the reason the third shot exists.*

**Definition.** After the serve, the receiving team must let it bounce before returning, and the serving team must let the return bounce before playing their next shot (the third shot). Only after those two bounces may either team volley.

**Why it matters.** The two-bounce rule is what stops the serving team from charging the net immediately behind the serve. It forces the serving team to play a third shot from the back of the court — which is *why* the third shot drop and the transition game exist at all. Take away this rule and pickleball's whole strategic structure collapses.

**Coaching it (adults).** Adults usually know the rule but don't connect it to *why the third shot is hard*. Make the link explicit: "the two-bounce rule is the reason you start the point behind — the third shot is how you climb out of that." It reframes the third shot from "a shot I keep missing" to "the shot the rules force me to have."

> Serve bounces, return bounces — then the game opens up.
