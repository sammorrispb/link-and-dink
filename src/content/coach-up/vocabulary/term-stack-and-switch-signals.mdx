# Stack-and-Switch Signals

*The communication layer that makes stacking, switching, and poaching work instead of producing collisions.*

**Definition.** The pre-point hand signals (often behind the back) and in-point verbal calls that doubles partners use to coordinate positioning — which side each will play, whether they're stacking, whether a switch or poach is coming.

**Why it matters.** Stacking, switching, and poaching all involve players moving across each other. Without a shared communication system, two partners end up in the same place with an open court next to them. The signals turn coordinated movement from a hopeful guess into a plan.

**Coaching it (adults).** Don't introduce signals until partners actually need them — they're an advanced layer on top of [stacking](term-stacking) and [poaching](term-poaching), and premature signals just add confusion. When you do, keep the system *minimal*: a small set of clear signals partners can actually remember under pressure beats an elaborate playbook nobody runs. The real lesson, again, is the [Creed](../creed/the-coach-up-creed)-level one — partners communicate, they don't assume.

> Signal before the serve, call during the point — no surprises between partners.
