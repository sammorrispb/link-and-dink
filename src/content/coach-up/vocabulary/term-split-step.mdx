# Split Step

*The smallest movement in the game, and the one that makes every other movement possible.*

**Definition.** A small, balanced hop that lands lightly on the balls of both feet, timed so the player is landing — loaded and neutral — at the moment the opponent contacts the ball. From that loaded position, the player can push off in any direction.

**Why it matters.** A player who is flat-footed when the opponent hits is always a half-beat late. The split step converts standing-still into ready-to-move. It's invisible when it's working and glaringly absent when it's not — a player who "can't get to balls" is very often a player who isn't split-stepping.

**Coaching it (adults).** This lives in block 1 of [the Adult Skill Progression](../frameworks/the-adult-skill-progression) — the athletic base — and you'll return to it constantly. The hard part is the *timing*, not the hop. Coach it externally: "land as they hit," not "hop now." Tie it to a cue the player can see (the opponent's contact), not an internal count.

> Land light just as they hit — be ready before the ball is.
