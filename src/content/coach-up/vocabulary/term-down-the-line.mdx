# Down the Line

*The change-of-direction shot. Higher risk, and exactly because of that, sometimes the right call.*

**Definition.** A shot played straight ahead, roughly parallel to the sideline, staying on the same side of the court. The counterpart to the cross-court shot.

**Why it matters.** Down-the-line crosses a higher part of the net and has less court to land in, so it carries less margin than cross-court. But it's a vital tactical tool: it catches an opponent who's shading toward the cross-court ball, it attacks a weaker opponent on a specific side, and it changes a predictable cross-court rhythm.

**Coaching it (adults).** The error is hitting down-the-line *by accident* — a dink that drifts straight because the player wasn't controlling direction. Coach it as a **deliberate choice**: a player should know they chose down-the-line and why (the opponent leaned, the matchup, the surprise). Tie it to [target selection](term-target-selection) and the read that justifies the risk.

> Down the line is a choice, not a default — make it on purpose.
