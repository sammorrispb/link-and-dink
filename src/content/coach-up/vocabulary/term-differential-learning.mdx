# Differential Learning

*A research finding that gives you permission to stop chasing one textbook stroke.*

**Definition.** A practice approach, associated with the researcher Wolfgang Schöllhorn, that deliberately introduces *variability* into movement — varying the task, the stance, the feed, the conditions so no two reps are identical — rather than repeating one "correct" form. The variability itself drives learning.

**Why it matters.** It challenges the deep coaching instinct to identify the One Ideal Technique and groove it. The research suggests that for many skills, *added variability* produces faster and more robust learning than grooved repetition — because the learner's system finds its own stable, adaptable solution.

**Coaching it (you, the coach).** Practically: vary your feed, vary the constraint, don't over-correct toward a single model stroke. It's the stronger cousin of [repetition without repetition](term-repetition-without-repetition). The mentor-log note "you're feeding too predictably — vary the angle every third ball" is this finding, applied. Use it as license to keep practice varied; see [Research Foundations](../creed/research-foundations).

> Vary the rep on purpose — there's no one perfect stroke to groove.
