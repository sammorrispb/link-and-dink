# Cross-Court

*The direction of the safe shot. When in doubt, this way.*

**Definition.** A shot played diagonally, landing on the opposite side of the court from where it was struck. Its counterpart is the down-the-line shot.

**Why it matters.** The cross-court shot travels over the lowest part of the net (the net sags in the middle) and has the most court to land in (the diagonal is longer than the straight line). That means more margin for error. For dinks especially, cross-court is the patient, percentage choice — it's the default ball in a steady dink rally.

**Coaching it (adults).** Make this a default a player can reach for under pressure: "when you're not sure, go cross-court — it's the higher-percentage ball." It pairs with [down-the-line](term-down-the-line) as a shot-selection decision: cross-court for safety and rally-building, down-the-line as a deliberate, situational change of direction. Teaching *why* cross-court is safer (the net, the geometry) lands better than just "hit it there."

> Cross-court is the safe ball — lower net, more court, more margin.
