# Kitchen (Non-Volley Zone)

*The defining feature of pickleball. The reason this isn't just small-court tennis.*

**Definition.** The 7-foot zone extending from the net on each side, in which a player may not strike a ball out of the air. A ball that bounces in the kitchen may be played; a ball volleyed while a player is touching the kitchen is a fault.

**Why it matters.** The non-volley zone is *why* the soft game exists. Without it, the player at the net would simply smash everything; the kitchen rule forces a softer, more patient, more tactical game built on dinks and resets. Understanding the kitchen isn't a rules footnote — it's understanding the strategic shape of the entire sport.

**Coaching it (adults).** Many adults — especially those from tennis — instinctively treat the kitchen as a danger zone to stay out of. Reframe it: the kitchen *line* is where you want to live, and the kitchen is where you're trying to make opponents play. The fear-of-the-kitchen habit is one of the first things to coach out.

> It's not a place to avoid — it's where the game is won.
